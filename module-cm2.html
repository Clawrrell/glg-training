💰 GLG Fee Structure — Know This Cold

Pre-litigation: 33.3% — applies when no lawsuit has been filed

Litigation: 40% — applies after a lawsuit is filed

On a $90,000 settlement: pre-lit fee = $30,000; lit fee = $36,000. The client's net changes by $6,000 depending on when they accept. Every client conversation about whether to accept or litigate requires this distinction.

The Specials Test: If the adjuster's offer does not clear the client's documented out-of-pocket losses (medical bills, lost wages, other specials), stop negotiating and escalate to the attorney immediately. An offer below specials is not a negotiation gap — it is a litigation signal.
No Zombie Negotiations: A stalled negotiation past its stage deadline must be escalated. Waiting without escalation is a process violation visible on the firm dashboard.

Pre-Negotiation Worksheet & Role Delineation

The attorney sets all parameters before the phone rings. The CM executes the plan. These roles never reverse.

📋 The Pre-Negotiation Worksheet — 7 Required Components

The attorney completes this worksheet BEFORE any first-offer call. A CM conducting negotiations without a completed worksheet is improvising with the client's money.

  1. Case facts FOR — key strengths: clear liability, treatment compliance, objective findings, documented wage loss, credible client
  2. Case facts AGAINST — weaknesses the adjuster will raise: treatment gaps, pre-existing conditions, credibility issues, prior accidents
  3. True floor — the minimum the attorney will accept; the CM must NEVER disclose this to the adjuster
  4. Pseudo-floor — the number the CM states as the apparent minimum; NOT the true floor; creates room between stated minimum and real minimum
  5. Concession increments — the planned sequence of counters (e.g., $65K → $58K → $52K → $48K); decreasing increments signal an approaching limit
  6. Walk-away trigger — the specific condition that authorizes filing suit
  7. Specials test threshold — the out-of-pocket loss minimum that must be cleared; if the adjuster will not clear specials, escalate immediately
Ripeness Check: Before any first-offer call, verify: Has the adjuster received the demand? Have they had time to review it? Do they have settlement authority? A call made before the other side is ready wastes leverage.

Four templates. Four distinct contexts. Using the wrong one at the wrong moment wastes leverage and time.

⚠️ Holt Demand — 5 Required Components

A Holt demand missing any element is NOT a valid Holt demand — it is an ordinary demand letter with a deadline and creates no additional legal leverage. Required components:

  1. Specific dollar amount — not a range
  2. Clear deadline — typically 30 days; must be a real deadline, not a placeholder
  3. Medical records attached — the carrier must have everything needed to evaluate and accept without further discovery
  4. Explicit expiration statement — the offer will not be renewed after the deadline
  5. Attorney authorization — explicit sign-off on every Holt demand, without exception
Filing is a one-way door: Once a lawsuit is filed, prior negotiations are off the table. The decision to file belongs to the attorney. A defective Holt that the carrier can challenge is a wasted litigation trigger.

Section 5 · The Client Side · Up to 40 pts

Net Projections, Written Acceptance & Release Documents

The settlement has two sides. The carrier side is the number. The client side is the experience. Both require their own protocol.

📊 Conservative Net Projection Formula

Run BEFORE asking the client to accept. The client decides on the conservative take-home number — not the gross offer.

Formula: Settlement − Attorney Fee − Case Costs − Estimated Lien Hold (with 15% cushion) = Conservative Net

Always project BELOW what you expect to deliver. The disbursement moment where the client receives MORE than promised is exactly the story they tell their network. "Under-promise, over-deliver" is a referral generation strategy embedded in every settlement conversation.

📋 Client-Side Template Suite

TemplateNamePurposeRecipient
#106Settlement_StatementFinancial breakdown: gross settlement, attorney fee, costs, lien holds, projected net — every deduction itemizedClient
#25Settlement_Release_CoverCover letter enclosing Template #109 for client signatureClient
#109General_Release_All_ClaimsThe binding release of the client's legal claims — rights extinguished on signature. Not reversible.Client
Three-Confirmation Standard before the client signs Template #109: (1) Client confirms they understand the gross settlement amount. (2) Client confirms they understand their net recovery (from Template #106). (3) Client affirmatively states they are satisfied with the resolution. A client who signs without understanding all three points has grounds to dispute — and the firm has no protective documentation.
Written acceptance is required: Text, email, or signed CasePeer memo — of the specific settlement number — BEFORE the CM confirms to the carrier. Verbal acceptance is not sufficient.

Section 6 · Settlement Documentation & Failure Modes · Up to 40 pts

Template #35, the Five Failure Modes & the Settlement Note

The settlement note is both the record and the handoff. It starts the post-settlement clock the moment it is logged.

📄 Template #35 — Settlement Confirmation to Adjuster

Template #35 (35_Settlement_Confirmation) is sent to the adjuster after written client acceptance is secured. It confirms the agreed settlement amount, identifies the case, and requests the carrier's release form and settlement check. Recipient: the adjuster — not the client.

🚫 The Five Banned Failure Modes — Prohibited by GLG's System

#Failure ModeWhat It Causes
1Slow counters — missing the 48-hour response clock on any roundAdjuster prices the firm as a low-urgency file; leverage lost
2No negotiation plan — CM initiating or conducting negotiations without the pre-negotiation worksheetImprovising with the client's money; floor exposure
3Verbal-only settlement — moving to Settled status based on oral confirmation without documented written client acceptanceClient can dispute; firm has no protective documentation
4Over-projecting net — quoting the client a take-home at or above expected disbursementClient disappointment; no referral story; trust damage
5Zombie negotiations — stalled negotiation past stage deadline without escalationAdjuster prices stall as a weak file; client's case held hostage

📝 The CasePeer Settlement Note — Required Contents

The settlement note must contain all five elements before the status changes to Settled:

  1. Settlement amount
  2. Date confirmed
  3. Template(s) used (e.g., "Template #35 sent to adjuster")
  4. Client acceptance documentation reference (e.g., "Client text confirmation 6/22/2026 3:14 PM")
  5. Attorney who approved

Handoff trigger: The Settled status change starts the post-settlement clock. Day 0 obligations begin the moment this note is logged and status changes.
