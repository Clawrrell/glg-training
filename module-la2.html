The LOR System — Why Templates Matter

GLG’s systems approach to legal correspondence.

⚖ The Systems Principle

GLG operates on a systems principle: quality should depend on the system, not on the individual at the keyboard. The firm’s 120+ CasePeer templates are that system. Every LOR, every HIPAA authorization, every records request — generated from a tested, compliant template so that output is consistent regardless of who produces it.

📋 The Intake Package — What Goes Out First

  • Within 48 hours of signing: Welcome letter, HIPAA Authorization, LOR to carrier(s)
  • Within 72 hours: Records requests to all identified providers
  • Every template has a number. Every number has one correct context. Verify before generating.
📋 Section First, Template Second. CasePeer organizes templates by the section of the case you are working in. Client letters → Client section. Insurance carrier letters → Defendants → Adjusters. Medical provider letters → Medical Treatment → Provider. Before generating any letter, confirm which section you are in — the section determines which templates are available.

Medical Records & Bills Templates

Precision matters. Request exactly what you need.

📋 Records & Bills Template Reference

TemplateNameUsed For
#04Full Medical Records RequestRequest all records AND bills from a provider
#05Bills Only RequestBilling statements only — no treatment notes
#06Medical Records OnlyTreatment notes, SOAP notes, clinical records — no bills
#07SOAP Notes OnlyNarrow request for just the clinical progress notes
#12Letter of Protection (LOP)Authorizes provider to defer billing until case settles
#14Treatment Gap LetterExplains a gap in treatment to the adjuster
#74Follow-Up Records Request14-day follow-up when provider has not responded
#75Updated Records RequestRequest for updated records from a provider who has already sent initial records
Specificity saves time. Sending a “Full Records Request” (#04) when you only need what is missing creates re-processing work for the provider and often delays the actual delivery. Use the most targeted template available.
Treatment gaps matter. A gap in treatment — even if the client just missed appointments — is something adjusters use to reduce demand value. Never file and forget. Flag every gap to the CM.

From Records to Demand — The Handoff Workflow

Your records work is what makes or breaks the demand.

🚨 LA Top Priority: Police Report → Insurance Info → LOR

Before records collection begins, there is a higher-priority workflow:

  1. Get the police report ASAP — Template #65 (Accident Report Request) or #66/#67 (Open Records). Coordinate with Intake — they check frequently too. Work together, don’t duplicate.
  2. Extract insurance information from the police report (carrier name, policy number)
  3. Send the LOR (#01 At-Fault via Defendants → Adjusters in CasePeer) to open the claim
  4. Carrier confirms three things: coverage, limits, and liability
🚨 ESCALATION TRIGGERS — Notify CMs and attorneys IMMEDIATELY via email when:
🔴 Coverage is DENIED
🔴 Liability is DENIED
🔴 Policy limits are $100,000 or higher
“Immediately” means same-day email. Do not wait for the next status update or team meeting. These change the trajectory of the case.

📋 The Demand Workflow at GLG

GLG uses an outside demand vendor to write demand narratives and assemble the final demand package. The primary vendor is Marley. If Marley is unavailable, the fallback is Trivent.

The LA does NOT draft demands. Your job is to build a complete records package — every medical record, every bill, every supporting document — so the demand writer has everything they need.

  1. LA collects all medical records and bills → logs each request and receipt in CasePeer
  2. When treatment is complete and all records received, LA assembles the records package
  3. LA hands off to Marley (or Trivent) → logs handoff in CasePeer
  4. Vendor writes the demand narrative and assembles the final package
  5. Demand returns to CM for review
  6. CM forwards to attorney for final review and approval
  7. After demand goes out, CM owns 30-day follow-up cadence

☑ Records Completeness Checklist (verify ALL before handoff)

  • All treating providers identified and cross-referenced with treatment timeline
  • Medical records received from EACH provider
  • Medical bills received from EACH provider (billed amounts)
  • Bills match records — no provider has bills without records or vice versa
  • ER visit providers separated: hospital, ER physician, radiology, ambulance (each bills independently)
  • Photos included (scene, vehicle damage, injuries)
  • Police/incident report included
  • Lost wage documentation if applicable
  • HIPAA authorization (#44) on file for every provider
  • All follow-up requests (#74) resolved — no outstanding records
  • Treatment gaps addressed — Template #14 sent if applicable
What the LA does NOT do: The LA does not draft demand narratives. The LA does not generate demand templates (#08/#09) from CasePeer — the vendor handles drafting. The LA does not send demands to carriers or make settlement decisions. Your demand-stage role is building a complete, accurate records package and handing it off.

📋 After the Demand — CM Workflow (Context)

The CM handles everything after the demand goes out:

  • CM receives completed demand from Marley/Trivent and reviews for accuracy
  • CM forwards to attorney for final review
  • CM sets 30-day follow-up (Template #22) in CasePeer
  • CM generates counteroffers (Template #23) when the carrier responds with a low number
  • CM ensures Holt language (Template #32) when attorney directs a statutory demand
  • Settlement templates (#109 Release, #106 Settlement Statement) used after case settles
Holt Demand Context: Template #32 is a time-limited statutory demand under O.C.G.A. § 9-11-67.1 with six required elements. The CM ensures Holt language is included; the attorney verifies. ONE missing element = invalid Holt demand = ZERO statutory penalties. Know what a Holt demand is — even though the CM and attorney handle execution.

CasePeer Template Workflow — Hands-On

Generate. Log. Follow up. Fix errors the right way.

📋 Template Workflow — Step by Step

  1. Navigate to the correct CasePeer section (verify the section before touching the template)
  2. Select template by number — confirm the full template name matches your intent
  3. Fill in all required fields: client name, carrier, adjuster, addresses, date
  4. Generate PDF — review it before sending
  5. Send via appropriate method (fax, mail, certified mail per template type)
  6. Log the send in CasePeer: date, recipient, template number used
  7. Set follow-up task (14 days for records; 30 days for demand)
Error rule: If a generated document has incorrect information, DELETE it and REGENERATE from scratch. Never manually edit template output with PDF tools, Word, or any other software. Template output is a firm document — you do not alter it.
If it is not logged in CasePeer, it did not happen. The send log is the firm’s documentation that the letter went out. No log = no record = no accountability.
