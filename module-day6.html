The Foundation

Why medical records matter — and what happens without them
📂 Context: How Cases Work

A personal injury case lives or dies on documentation. The adjuster doesn't know what happened to your client — you have to prove it.

Medical records are that proof. They show what injuries the client suffered, what treatment they received, how long they treated, and what it cost. Every demand letter, every negotiation, every settlement is built on this foundation.

📋 GLG Medical Records Escalation Ladder

14

Day 14 — First Follow-Up
LA calls or messages provider. Confirms receipt of request. Gets ETA. Logs call in CasePeer.
30
Case Manager Flagged
Day 30 — CM Escalation
LA flags CM. CM reviews case. LA continues follow-up every 5–7 days and documents each attempt.
60
Case Manager Direct
Day 60 — CM Calls Directly
CM personally contacts provider. May escalate to records retrieval service. All attempts logged.
90
Attorney / Legal Action
Day 90 — Attorney + Potential Subpoena
Attorney involved. Subpoena may be issued. Records retrieval service engaged if not already. Provider is in violation.
