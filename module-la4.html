CasePeer Navigation & Daily Routine

The daily habits that keep every case moving.
Why This Matters

It's 9:02 AM Tuesday. Your dashboard has 3 overdue tasks from yesterday. Your paralegal is in a meeting. A client called at 5:48 PM yesterday and is waiting for a callback. Where do you start?

Everything feels urgent. Nothing has a clear order. This section gives you the daily discipline that prevents that feeling from ever happening again. CasePeer is only a system of record if you treat it like one — every morning, every task, every single day.

⚡ The Daily CasePeer Principle

When you log in each morning, your dashboard shows what needs to happen today and what was overdue from yesterday. The sequence is not discretionary: overdue tasks first. Not after email. Not after coffee. First.

At end of day, when you close your laptop: every communication is logged, every task is set, every case status is current. No exceptions. That is the daily close-out standard.

Communication Notes & Logging Standards

The record that proves the firm worked — or the gap that proves it didn't.
Why This Matters

Eighteen months from now, a client insists the firm never called after their settlement was reached. The case goes to the attorney. She opens CasePeer and finds: "Called client — discussed offer." That one note is now a malpractice inquiry.

Thorough logging is not a compliance exercise. It is the written record that proves the system worked — or exposes the gap where it broke. Every note you write is a hand-off package for whoever picks up this case next.

📝 Required Format for Every Communication Note

DATE | METHOD | WHO | WHAT | NEXT STEPS

The handoff test: Could a colleague who has never touched this file read your note and pick up where you left off — without calling you? If yes, it's a good note. If no, rewrite it.

  • WHO: Full name, title, phone number of the person you spoke with
  • WHAT: Specific topics discussed — not vague summaries
  • DECIDED: What was agreed or learned
  • NEXT STEPS: Every commitment gets a deadline and a task in CasePeer

Document Uploads & The Dual-Upload Rule

OneDrive has the file. CasePeer has the data. Both are required. Always.
Why This Matters

Records from Dr. Martinez arrived Tuesday. You uploaded them to OneDrive. Thursday, the demand meeting starts. The attorney asks: "Are all records confirmed in CasePeer?" You check — it still says "Requested." The demand is delayed. The client waits longer. You know exactly which step you skipped.

OneDrive stores the documents. CasePeer stores the data — provider name, bill amounts, received dates, lien types. Both must be updated. Doing one and skipping the other is not "half done." It is incomplete documentation.

📂 The Dual-Upload Rule (Non-Negotiable)

When you receive medical records or bills, you MUST do BOTH:

  1. Upload the documents to OneDrive — in the client's case folder
  2. Enter the information in CasePeer — provider, dates, amounts, lien type

If you only do one, the information is incomplete. This is not a preference — it is a compliance requirement. Expenses are entered in CasePeer's Costs section at the time they are incurred — not retroactively. Receipts go to OneDrive the same day.

Templates, Case Status & Task Management

Right template. Right status. Right time. Every case depends on it.
Why This Matters

The adjuster accepted the demand. CasePeer still reads "Active Treatment." Two colleagues look at the same pipeline report and think two different things about where this case stands. A letter goes out twice. A task gets created twice. A client gets two conflicting calls.

Case status accuracy is not cosmetic. It is the shared language the entire firm uses to see the case. Wrong status creates false bottlenecks, duplicated work, and missed deadlines — none of which the client can see, but all of which they eventually feel.

📋 CasePeer Status Definitions (Pre-Lit)

  • New / Intake — Case just signed
  • Active Treatment — Client actively treating with providers
  • Records Retrieval — Gathering records and bills; treatment complete
  • Demand Preparation — Building the demand package
  • Demand Sent — Demand delivered to adjuster, awaiting response
  • Negotiation — Active offers and counters in progress
  • Settlement / Disbursement — Settled, processing payout
  • Closed — Case complete

Rule: Update the status every time the case moves to a new phase. Leaving "Active Treatment" on a case where the demand has been sent is not a minor error — it corrupts every pipeline report the firm generates.

Data Accuracy, Self-Monitoring & Quality Control

Know where you stand before your supervisor does.
Why This Matters

Your monthly scorecard arrives. Two red flags — CasePeer fields that have been blank for 47 days. Since the file was opened. You never noticed because no one told you to check. Now someone else noticed first.

Self-monitoring is not a preference or a nice-to-have. It is a professional expectation. You pull your own reports once a week. You know your flags before your supervisor does. You fix them before they show up on the scorecard. That is the standard.

📊 Key Self-Monitoring Standards

  • CasePeer data accuracy: ≥ 95% — audited monthly
  • Self-monitoring frequency: At least once per week — pull reports yourself
  • Records follow-up cadence: Every 14 days — no exceptions, no gaps
  • Missing data fields to watch: Policy limits, liability assessment, property damage status, UM/UIM coverage — fill these immediately if blank
  • Error correction: Self-report data errors to the paralegal the same business day — do not wait for an audit to surface them
