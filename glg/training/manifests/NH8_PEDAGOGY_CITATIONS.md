## Section-by-Section Pedagogy Rationale

### Section 1 — Intro (No points)
**Pedagogy:** Talk to the Elephant (visceral opener), Art of Game Design (interest curve hook)  
**Design:** Opens with client who has been waiting 6 weeks for their check — makes the emotional stakes immediate. System 1 principle embedded: "Follow-up means a phone call."  
**SOP Source:** Post_Settlement_SOP.md §§1, 6

### Section 2 — What Are Liens? (Q1–Q4, 40 pts)
**Pedagogy:** Make It Stick (retrieval), Badass (micro-skill: lien identification), ADDIE (audience = zero prior knowledge)  
**Design:** Introduces 5 lien types as the foundational taxonomy. Questions test identification, not definition-recall. Scenario-based (Maria Santos, DOL 3/12/2025).  
**SOP Sources:** Hospital_Lien_Quick_Guide.md §§1-3, Post_Settlement_SOP.md §§2-3

### Section 3 — The GSCCCA Search (Q5–Q8, 40 pts)
**Pedagogy:** Action Mapping (key behavior: check GSCCCA before holding funds), Make It Stick (testing effect), Interface Design for Learning (cognitive load — decision tree format)  
**Design:** Forces the filed-lien vs. billing-claim distinction. GSCCCA search is a specific observable action. Aspirion/collection letter scenario (common real error).  
**SOP Sources:** Hospital_Lien_Verification_Guide.md, Hospital_Lien_Verification_Checklist.md

### Section 4 — Hospital Lien Rules (Q9–Q12, 40 pts)
**Pedagogy:** Make It Stick (interleaving — statute rules mixed with real examples), Essential Lawyering (backward mapping — work from outcome back to procedural validity), Nesta (expertise hierarchy — novice needs rules first)  
**Design:** Tests the 5 Georgia hospital lien rules from O.C.G.A. §§ 44-14-470 through 44-14-476. Scenario-based with real provider types (Grady, Emory). Mix of "which rule applies" and "what action to take."  
**SOP Sources:** Hospital_Lien_Quick_Guide.md (Rules 1-6)

### Section 5 — Post-Settlement Timeline (Q13–Q16, 40 pts)
**Pedagogy:** Map It (business goal = 90%+ files closed in 45 days → actions = Day 0 notification, 48h reduction requests, 7-day follow-up), Talk to Elephant (urgency framing), GoBeyond (practice loops → the timeline IS the loop)  
**Design:** Tests timeline compliance as behavioral standards, not memory. What do you do on Day 0? Day 7? What triggers escalation?  
**SOP Sources:** Post_Settlement_SOP.md §§1, 4, 7

### Section 6 — Reduction & Disbursement (Q17–Q20, 40 pts)
**Pedagogy:** Badass (micro-skill: partial disbursement calculation, pro-rata reduction), Essential Lawyering (four challenges in counseling — the net-to-client explanation), E-Myth (system runs the disbursement, not the individual)  
**Design:** Tests partial disbursement calculation (hold lien + 10% cushion, disburse rest NOW), pro-rata reduction argument, Made Whole doctrine basics for NH audience.  
**SOP Sources:** Lien_Negotiation_Guide.md §§1-3, Post_Settlement_SOP.md §§3-5, Health_Insurance_Reimbursement_Guide.md §§4-5

### Section 7 — Boss Challenge (Q21–Q25, up to 50 bonus pts)
**Pedagogy:** Art of Game Design (Boss challenge = climactic high-difficulty moment), Make It Stick (generation effect — open-ended scenario forces synthesis), Badass (expert pattern recognition across multiple lien types on one case)  
**Design:** Mario Reyes case (GLG-2026-1209) — multi-lien scenario with hospital lien, health insurance lien, and Medicare. 5 questions testing integrated judgment.  
**SOP Sources:** All 7 SOPs + System 1 (escalation principle)
