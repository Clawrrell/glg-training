## Content Decisions (Pedagogy Applications)

**Make It Stick:** All 20 questions require retrieval, not recognition. Progressive difficulty. Spaced across 6 sections.

**Art of Game Design:** Interest curve opens with high-stakes scenario (client waiting 6 weeks for check). Boss challenge escalates stakes. Points toast on correct answers.

**Action Mapping:** Every question traces to a specific SOP action. No trivia — all real workflow decisions.

**Badass / Competence:** NH-8 builds the micro-skills of: lien identification → GSCCCA verification → timeline compliance → partial disbursement → escalation. Each is independently testable.

**Talk to Elephant:** Intro scenario triggers visceral urgency (client call 3, 6 weeks no money). Feedback frames consequences to the client's experience, not abstract rules.

**ADDIE:** Analysis → NH audience has zero prior lien knowledge (Days 1-7 were intake/records/coverage). Design → intro-level, not advanced. Development → chassis graft from NH-7. Evaluation → 80% threshold (160/200).

**E-Myth / System 1:** Module embeds "Fix it once" and "Follow-up means a phone call" as correct answers, not options.
