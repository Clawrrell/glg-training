## Section-Level Pedagogy Architecture

### Overall Structure (ADDIE + Action Mapping)
- **Analysis:** LA audience has LA-1 (records retrieval), LA-2 (LOR generation), LA-3 (lien packets) — LA-4 adds the operational backbone tying all prior work to CasePeer
- **Design:** 5 content sections + Boss Challenge; each section covers a distinct skill domain; difficulty escalates from "navigation/routine" to "quality control" to "full inherited-file audit"
- **Development:** LA-3 chassis with LA-4 content; dynamic sectionMax; scenario-first ordering per section
- **Implementation:** Self-paced in-browser; mobile-optimized; Google Sheets score tracking
- **Evaluation:** Boss Challenge = performance-level test (Competence under realistic conditions)

## Make It Stick Principles Applied

| Principle | Where Applied |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Retrieval Practice** | All 20 base questions + 5 boss questions require active recall of procedures, not recognition of definitions |
| **Generation Effect** | Q8 (calibration): "Before answering, think through what you know about...then answer" — generates the answer before seeing options |
| **Calibration Check** | Q8 Section 2 + Q20 Section 5 explicitly flagged as calibration prompts; trainee must self-assess confidence before committing |
| **Interleaving** | Boss Challenge (Section 6) interleaves concepts from all 5 prior sections in a single inherited-file scenario |
| **Elaboration** | Feedback text explains the WHY behind every correct answer and traces it to the specific SOP line |
| **Spacing** | LA-4 follows LA-1 through LA-3; distributed across the onboarding timeline rather than crammed in one session |
