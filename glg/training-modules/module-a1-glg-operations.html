The Systems Foundation

Why Systems Matter More Than Talent

🎯 Core Principle: "You don't rise to the level of your goals — you fall to the level of your systems." GLG runs on systems, not heroics. When the system works, clients win consistently.

Most law firms fail because they're organized around the owner's personal involvement. The attorney does everything. When they're out, nothing happens. That's not a business — it's a job.

GLG is built differently. We operate on the Franchise Prototype model. Every process is documented. Every role has clear accountability. The firm works whether Harrell is here or not.

The Three Personalities in Every Business

The Entrepreneur (Visionary)

Dreams big, sets direction, drives change. At GLG: Harrell sets the vision and strategy.

The Manager (Integrator)

Creates order, builds systems, ensures accountability. At GLG: Our operations team and case managers.

The Technician (Doer)

Does the actual work — legal research, client calls, document prep. At GLG: Every team member executes their role.

The key insight: All three must work together. When staff just "do the work" without understanding the system, cases stall. When there's vision but no management, nothing gets executed. GLG balances all three.

Working ON the Business, Not IN It

🔧 Every GLG team member works ON the business by following systems that improve client outcomes. You're not just processing cases — you're building a machine that serves injured Georgians.

When you document every case note in CasePeer, you're working ON the business. When you follow the callback SOP exactly, you're working ON the business. These systems compound over time into competitive advantage.

Building Winning Habits

The Power of 1% Improvements

📈 Core Principle: "You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems." Small daily improvements compound into extraordinary results.

A 1% daily improvement leads to 37x better performance over one year. Conversely, 1% daily decline compounds toward zero. At GLG, every small action either builds toward client success or undermines it.

The GLG Habit Loop

Every reliable behavior follows the same pattern: Cue → Craving → Response → Reward

Cue: Make It Obvious

Environmental triggers that prompt good behavior. CasePeer notifications, callback logs on your desk, daily huddle check-ins.

Craving: Make It Attractive

The motivation to act. Recognition for compliance, team celebrations, knowing you're helping injured clients.

Response: Make It Easy

The actual behavior. Templates that take 30 seconds, one-click CasePeer updates, streamlined call scripts.

Reward: Make It Satisfying

The payoff that reinforces the loop. Green scorecard metrics, client thank-you notes, performance bonuses.

Implementation at GLG

🎯 Habit Stacking: "After I update a case note in CasePeer, I will check if any callbacks are due before closing the tab." Link new habits to existing ones.

The Two-Minute Rule: When establishing a new system, start small. "Check callback log" becomes "Open callback log." Once the habit of showing up is established, improvement follows naturally.

Never Miss Twice: One missed callback is an error. Two in a row is the start of a bad habit. The scorecard catches these patterns before they become entrenched.

Identity-Based Habits

🏆 "Every action is a vote for the type of person you want to become." At GLG, we ARE a firm that communicates with clients, follows systems, and delivers results. That's our identity.

Don't think "I need to hit my callback targets." Think "I am a case manager who never lets clients wonder if we forgot them." Identity drives behavior more powerfully than goals.

PI Operations Excellence

Core Legal Practice Principles

⚖️ Core Insight: "Excellent lawyering is built on interviewing, counseling, negotiation, and persuasive fact analysis — not just knowing the law." GLG staff apply these same principles.

You don't have to be an attorney to use these frameworks. Every client interaction — intake calls, case updates, settlement discussions — benefits from professional communication skills.

Inquiring Mode vs. Persuasion Mode

Inquiring Mode

When to use: Learning about client's situation, understanding concerns, gathering case facts.

How it sounds: "Tell me what happened after the accident." "What's your biggest concern right now?"

Persuasion Mode

When to use: Explaining process, presenting options, guiding decisions.

How it sounds: "Here's what we recommend next." "Based on cases like yours, this is typical."

The mistake: Jumping to persuasion mode too quickly. Always understand the client's real concerns before explaining solutions.

The Four Challenges in Client Counseling

Whether you're discussing settlement offers, treatment plans, or case timelines:

  1. Create options beyond the obvious first choice
  2. Work out each option precisely with real numbers and timelines
  3. Explain clearly so the client truly understands
  4. Maintain professional empathy — caring but intellectually detached

CasePeer as Documentation System

💻 Non-negotiable rule: If it's not documented in CasePeer, it didn't happen. Every call, email, and case action must be logged immediately.

This protects the client, the firm, and you. Six months from now, nobody will remember the details of a phone call. CasePeer will.
