GLG Systems Understanding — Can You Explain It?

E-Myth Self-Assessment

The E-Myth test: Can you explain GLG's systems to someone else? If you understand the "why" behind each process, you can teach it, adapt it, and improve it. If you only know the steps without understanding the purpose, you're operating as a Technician, not building systems mastery.

✅ Can You Explain These GLG Systems?

Why we send LORs within 24-48 hours
The purpose, not just the rule. Why does timing matter? What happens if we wait?
How the 14-day follow-up rule prevents case stalls
Why every 14 days? What's the system logic? How does it serve clients?
The difference between LOR and LOP and when to use each
Different recipients, different purposes. Can you explain this to a client?
Why we always request records AND bills together
The strategic reason, not just the procedure. How does this support case value?
How CasePeer templates maintain GLG's professional standard
Why templates exist, what they accomplish, how they serve the client experience.
The role of medical liens in settlement disbursement
What LOPs create, why liens must be tracked, how lien reduction works.

Client-Centered Review

Every GLG system exists to serve the client experience. LORs protect clients from adjuster pressure. The 14-day rule ensures their case doesn't stall. Templates ensure professional communication. Understanding the client benefit in each system shows you're thinking like an owner, not just following rules.

⚠️ If You Can't Explain It

If any of these feel unclear, that's valuable feedback. It means you need to go deeper on that system before moving to Week 2. Better to identify gaps now than discover them when a client asks for clarification.

Section 3

Your 90-Day Rocks — New Hire Development Plan

Traction EOS: Rocks for New Hires

Rocks are your 3-7 most important priorities for the next 90 days. As a new hire, your Rocks should focus on: (1) mastering core systems, (2) building relationships with the team, (3) demonstrating reliability in key processes, and (4) identifying areas where you can add value to GLG's operations.

🎯 Your Next 90 Days at GLG

Atomic Habits Integration

Rocks are outcomes. Habits are the daily actions that achieve them. Each Rock should be supported by specific daily and weekly habits. For example, "Systems Mastery" might require: daily CasePeer review practice, weekly discussion with your supervisor, and monthly self-assessment of progress.

✅ Making Rocks Measurable

Good Rocks have clear success criteria. Instead of "get better at communication," try "complete all client callbacks within 24 hours with documented outcomes in CasePeer." Specific, measurable Rocks create accountability and show progress.

Section 4

Daily Habits That Build Excellence

Atomic Habits: 1% Better Every Day

Small habits, compounded over time, produce remarkable results. As a new GLG team member, building the right daily habits now will determine your long-term success. Focus on habits that make the right behavior automatic, not forced.

🔄 Daily Habit Stack for Legal Assistants

  • Morning startup: "After I log into CasePeer, I will review my callback log and set today's priorities."
  • Case management: "After I complete a client call, I will immediately log the outcome in CasePeer before taking the next action."
  • Records tracking: "After I send a records request, I will set a 14-day follow-up task before closing the case tab."
  • End-of-day review: "Before logging out, I will review tomorrow's tasks and confirm all follow-ups are scheduled."
  • Quality check: "After I complete a template, I will review it once for accuracy before sending."
⚡
Start with 2-minute habits
Begin with the smallest version of each habit. "Review callback log" instead of "complete all callbacks." Master the showing-up part first, then expand.
🔗
Stack new habits on existing ones
Anchor new behaviors to established routines. "After I check email" or "After I log into CasePeer" provide natural cues for new habits.
📊
Track your streak
Visual tracking creates satisfaction and urgency. A simple checklist or calendar X marks can motivate consistency better than complex systems.
❌
Never miss twice
One missed day is a mistake. Two consecutive missed days is the start of a new (bad) habit. Get back on track immediately.

⚠️ Habit Formation Reality Check

New habits feel unnatural at first. That's normal. The goal is to make compliance with GLG systems feel automatic after 90 days. Expect resistance for the first 2-3 weeks, then gradual ease, then automation.

✅ Environmental Design

Set up your workspace to make good habits obvious and bad habits invisible. Keep callback logs visible, set CasePeer bookmarks, use desk reminders for follow-ups. Your environment should cue the behavior you want.

Section 5

Asking for & Receiving Feedback at GLG

Radical Candor: Receiving Feedback as Growth Data

Feedback is information, not judgment. High performers actively seek feedback because it accelerates learning. At GLG, asking "What's one thing I could do better?" signals growth mindset and helps you improve faster than waiting for formal reviews.

🗣️ How to Ask for Feedback at GLG

  • Be specific: "How did I handle that client call?" not "How am I doing?"
  • Ask regularly: Weekly check-ins, not just when problems arise
  • Listen without defending: Summarize what you heard before responding
  • Thank the feedback-giver: Even difficult feedback helps you improve
  • Follow up: Report back on changes you made based on their input
❓
Go-To Questions for Your Supervisor
"What's one thing I could do to be more effective in my role?" or "Which of my current projects would benefit most from your input?"
👂
Active Listening Process
Listen → Summarize what you heard → Ask clarifying questions → Thank them → Identify specific actions to take.
🔄
Close the Loop
Follow up within a week: "You suggested I [specific action]. Here's what I implemented and the result I'm seeing."
🎯
Make It Safe for Others to Be Honest
Never punish honest feedback with defensiveness or arguments. People stop giving useful feedback when it creates conflict.

Growth Mindset at GLG

Replace "I don't know how" with "I don't know how yet." GLG values team members who see challenges as learning opportunities. Asking for help isn't weakness — it's professional development and shows you care about getting things right.

✅ Weekly Feedback Rhythm

Schedule brief weekly check-ins with your supervisor for the first 90 days. Come prepared with specific questions, updates on your Rocks, and examples of what you've learned. This creates a feedback loop that accelerates your development.

Feedback isn't criticism. It's coaching.

The best performers actively seek it because it shortens the learning curve.
