What Is a Lien — and Who Can File One?

Five lien types. Every settled case. Know them cold.
📋 Maria Santos — Case Summary

Maria Santos, rear-end collision, DOL March 12, 2025. Case just settled for $55,000.

Maria treated at WellStar Kennestone (hospital) and a chiropractor. She had Blue Cross Blue Shield health insurance through her employer. Medicare paid for some treatment at the end. And GLG has an attorney fee agreement. That's at least four parties who may have a claim on this settlement.

🏥 Hospital / Provider Lien

Filed by hospitals, physician practices, chiropractors, nursing homes, or burn care centers under Georgia law. Must be filed with the Clerk of Superior Court. Verified on GSCCCA.org.

🔵 Health Insurance Subrogation

When the client's health insurer paid their bills and wants repayment from the settlement. Whether Georgia's Made Whole Doctrine applies depends on plan type (fully insured vs. ERISA self-funded).

🟢 Medicare Lien

Federal program. Federal right of recovery. MUST be addressed before disbursement. Contact BCRC at 1-855-798-2627 to get a conditional payment letter. Personal attorney liability if ignored.

🟣 Medicaid Lien

State/federal program. Georgia Medicaid has automatic right of reimbursement under O.C.G.A. § 49-4-149. Contact GA Dept of Community Health. Subject to Made Whole doctrine in some circumstances.

⚖️ Attorney Lien

GLG's fee and costs. Under O.C.G.A. § 44-14-470(b), attorney liens have PRIORITY over hospital liens — attorney fee comes out before the hospital gets paid. This matters at negotiation time.

Filed Lien vs. Billing Claim

GSCCCA.org is the only authoritative source. A demand letter is not a lien.
📋 Maria Santos — Day After Settlement

Settlement confirmed. You've just received a letter from Aspirion Healthcare claiming to represent WellStar and demanding $22,000 from the settlement.

Before you do anything — before you hold funds, before you call Aspirion, before you respond — there is one step that changes everything. You need to know whether WellStar actually filed a lien with the court.

🔍 The Filed Lien vs. Billing Claim Distinction

Filed Lien: The provider went to the Clerk of Superior Court and filed a formal lien under O.C.G.A. § 44-14-470. This appears on GSCCCA.org. You are legally required to hold funds to satisfy it.

Billing Claim: The provider has a debt the client owes — but no court filing. No lien = no legal obligation to hold settlement funds. The client can pay the provider directly (or not). Use the Self-Pay Addendum.

⚠️ Aspirion, Synergi, and Collection Companies

Companies like Aspirion and Synergi send demand letters on behalf of hospitals. These letters are NOT filed liens. A letter claiming "we represent WellStar" does not create a court-filed lien. Always verify on GSCCCA before holding any funds.

📊 GSCCCA.org — How to Search

  1. Go to www.gsccca.org
  2. Click "Search Records" → "Lien Search"
  3. Select the county where the hospital is located
  4. Search by client name (Last, First)
  5. Look for "Hospital Lien" or "Medical Lien" entries
  6. Note filing date, amount, provider name

5 Rules That Make or Break a Hospital Lien

Georgia law creates specific requirements. Miss one — the lien is void.
📋 Maria Santos — Lien Verification

WellStar filed a lien. Before you negotiate or pay anything, you need to know if this lien is actually valid under Georgia law. Run the five-point verification checklist.

Under O.C.G.A. § 44-14-471(b): "Failure of any person or institution to comply with the requirements of this Code section shall render the lien of such person or institution unenforceable." One missed requirement = the lien is gone.

📋 Five Georgia Hospital Lien Rules (O.C.G.A. §§ 44-14-470 to 44-14-476)

#RuleStatute
1 Authorized filers only: Hospitals, nursing homes, physician practices, chiropractic practices, traumatic burn care centers. Nothing else. § 44-14-470
2 Certified mail notice: Provider must send certified mail notice to patient, at-fault party, and attorney at least 15 days before filing. § 44-14-471(a)(1)
3 Filing deadlines: Hospitals/nursing homes/burn care = 75 days from discharge. Physician/chiro practices = 90 days from treatment. § 44-14-471(a)(2)
4 2023 Insurance Rule: Hospital must first submit claim to client's health insurer AND receive a rejection before asserting a lien. If insurer paid part, lien limited to what wasn't paid. § 44-14-471(c)
5 Enforcement deadline: Provider must take enforcement action within 1 year of settlement. Miss this — lien is extinguished. § 44-14-473(a)

Reducing Liens & Getting Clients Their Money

Every dollar reduced from a lien goes straight to the client. This is one of the most impactful things you'll do.
📋 Maria Santos — Lien Negotiation Phase

WellStar's billing department says "full payment only." Blue Cross wants reimbursement. Medicare has a conditional payment. Time to put the reduction arguments to work.

On a $55,000 settlement with $22,000 in liens, negotiating those liens down to $11,000 means an extra $11,000 in Maria's pocket. Every dollar reduced from a lien is a dollar that goes to the client.

⚖️ The Two Core Reduction Arguments

1. Pro-Rata Reduction: When the settlement doesn't fully compensate the client, all providers should reduce their liens proportionally. The client was not made whole — every provider shares in the shortfall.

2. Common Fund Doctrine: The attorney's work created the fund from which the lien will be paid. The provider should reduce by the attorney fee percentage (33.3%) — they benefit from the attorney's effort without contributing to it.

🔵 Made Whole Doctrine — Fully Insured Health Plans

Under Georgia law (Duncan v. Integon, 267 Ga. 646), a fully insured health plan CANNOT enforce its subrogation lien unless the client has been "made whole" — fully compensated for ALL losses. If the settlement doesn't cover all of Maria's damages, Blue Cross may get nothing. Send the Made Whole Letter (Template 26) and give them 30 days to respond.

Important: This doctrine does NOT apply to ERISA self-funded plans, Medicare, or Medicaid.
