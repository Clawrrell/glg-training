Know Your Lien Types

Not every claim against the settlement is the same.
📋 Darnell Carter — Post-Settlement

You've got the settlement check deposited in the trust account. Now you're staring at a stack of claims. Some are filed liens with legal power. Some are just bills. Some are from collection companies. Each one requires a different response.

Get this wrong and either the client waits months for money he's legally entitled to, or you disburse funds the firm was legally required to hold. Both are serious problems.

🏥 Hospital Liens

Filed with the court under O.C.G.A. § 44-14-470. Only specific providers can file: hospitals, nursing homes, physician practices, chiropractic practices, burn care centers. Must follow strict procedural rules or it's void.

🛡️ Insurance Subrogation

Health insurer demands reimbursement for bills they paid. Amount depends on plan type: fully-insured (Made Whole applies) vs. self-funded ERISA (federal law controls). This is where negotiation gets powerful.

⚕️ Medicare Liens

Federal right of recovery — MUST be addressed. Contact BCRC (1-855-798-2627). Procurement cost reduction applies (typically 1/3). Never disburse without resolving Medicare. Personal liability for the attorney.

🏛️ Medicaid Liens

State/federal program with statutory recovery rights under O.C.G.A. § 49-4-149. Contact GA Dept of Community Health. Must be addressed, but can be negotiated. Made Whole doctrine applies in Georgia.

📄 ERISA Plan Claims

Self-funded employer health plans. Federal ERISA law preempts Georgia's Made Whole Doctrine. Plan document controls. But Common Fund Doctrine still works — plan pays its share of attorney fees.

📬 Billing Claims (NOT Liens)

Collection letters from companies like Aspirion or Synergi claiming to represent hospitals. If no lien was filed with the court, this is just a billing claim. You are NOT required to hold settlement funds for it.

The 5-Step Lien Verification

A defective lien = money back to your client.
📋 Darnell Carter — Lien Investigation

You confirmed Grady's lien on GSCCCA. But confirming a lien EXISTS isn't the same as confirming it's VALID. Georgia law has strict procedural requirements — if ANY one is violated, the lien is automatically void.

This is where you save the client real money. Every defective lien you catch is money that stays in Darnell's pocket.

📋 Hospital Lien Verification Checklist

☐ Step 0: Is the filer authorized? (hospital, nursing home, physician practice, chiropractic practice, or burn care center) — O.C.G.A. § 44-14-470
☐ Step 1: Was the lien filed within 75 days of discharge? (hospitals/nursing homes) or 90 days (physician/chiropractic practices) — § 44-14-471(a)(2)
☐ Step 2: Was certified mail notice sent at least 15 days BEFORE filing? Regular mail does NOT count. — § 44-14-471(a)(1)
☐ Step 3: Did the provider bill the client's health insurance FIRST and receive a rejection? (2023 Rule) — § 44-14-471(c)
☐ Step 4: Has enforcement action been commenced within 1 year of settlement? — § 44-14-473(a)
🔑 Critical Rule (§ 44-14-471(b)): Failure to comply with ANY of these requirements renders the lien automatically unenforceable. You don't need a court order to challenge it. The statute does the work for you.
💰 Attorney Lien Priority (§ 44-14-470(b)): Attorney's liens have PRIORITY over hospital liens. Your firm's fee comes out first, before the hospital gets paid from settlement proceeds.

The Critical Threshold Question

Self-funded or fully-insured? The answer changes everything.
📋 Darnell Carter — Insurance Investigation

BCBS sent a subrogation letter demanding $22,000 — the amount they paid for Darnell's treatment. But before you respond to that letter, you need to answer one question that controls your entire negotiation strategy.

Is Darnell's BCBS plan self-funded by his employer, or fully insured through BCBS?

If fully insured → Georgia's Made Whole Doctrine may eliminate the lien entirely.
If self-funded → ERISA preempts Georgia law, and you'll need a different strategy.

✅ Fully Insured

Georgia law applies. Made Whole Doctrine: insurer can't collect until client is fully compensated for ALL losses. If settlement < total damages → insurer may get NOTHING. Send the Made Whole Letter.

⚠️ Self-Funded (ERISA)

Federal ERISA preempts Georgia law. Made Whole Doctrine does NOT apply. Plan document controls. BUT: Common Fund Doctrine works — plan pays pro-rata share of attorney fees (typically 33.3% reduction).

🔍 How to Identify the Plan Type

  • Call the number on the back of Darnell's insurance card
  • Ask: "Is this plan self-funded by the employer, or fully insured through BCBS?"
  • Request the Summary Plan Description (SPD) — look for "self-funded" or "self-insured" language
  • Check Form 5500 on dol.gov/efast for additional confirmation
  • When in doubt: treat as self-funded and research further before conceding the lien
💡 Made Whole Math: Darnell's total damages — past/future medical ($55,200), lost wages ($8,400), pain and suffering (at least $50,000) = $113,600+ in total damages. Settlement was $85,000. He was NOT made whole. If the plan is fully insured, BCBS may get zero.

Every Dollar You Reduce Is a Dollar for the Client

Lien negotiation is one of the most impactful things you do.
📋 Darnell Carter — Negotiation Time

Here's where you stand. Darnell's Grady lien might be defective (they didn't bill BCBS first). Piedmont Orthopedics has a $12,000 lien that appears valid. BCBS's $22,000 subrogation claim is against a fully-insured plan. Time to negotiate.

Every dollar you reduce from these claims goes directly into Darnell's pocket. On a $50K settlement with $20K in liens, negotiating down to $10K means an extra $10,000 for the client.

🛠️ GLG's Seven-Stage Lien Negotiation Framework

  1. Preparation: Know total settlement, all liens, fees/costs, projected net-to-client. Calculate the "made whole" threshold.
  2. Initial Contact: Send reduction requests within 48 hours of settlement. State total settlement, all other liens, and the reasonable reduction sought.
  3. Anchor High: Request the maximum reduction first — 1/3 is a starting point, not a ceiling.
  4. Procurement Cost Argument: Every lienholder should reduce by the attorney fee percentage (33.3%). They didn't create the fund — we did.
  5. Made-Whole Doctrine: If liens + fees exceed recovery, document and assert the made-whole argument. (Georgia fully-insured plans.)
  6. Loss Aversion Framing: "Would you prefer 40 cents on the dollar now, or risk zero if the client pursues litigation?"
  7. Escalation: If the lienholder refuses, escalate to Pre-Lit Head Attorney. Never disburse without resolution.

📊 Pro-Rata Reduction — The Math

Settlement$85,000
Attorney Fees (33.3%)−$28,305
Case Costs−$3,200
Available for Bills/Liens$53,495
Total Claims$77,200
Pro-Rata Percentage$53,495 ÷ $77,200 = 69.3%

Each provider's reduction offer = their claim × 69.3%. This is your starting position — many will accept it because the alternative is waiting longer for less certainty.

The Clock Is Ticking

Every day a file sits open is a day the client is waiting.
📋 Darnell Carter — Disbursement Phase

Darnell is calling every day. "Where's my check?" This is the #1 complaint clients have after settlement. The post-settlement SOP exists to close the gap between settlement and check-in-hand.

Your target: 90%+ of files closed within 45 days. Every step has a deadline. Every delay must be documented and escalated.

D0
Day 0: Settlement Confirmed
Log in CasePeer → Status: Settled. Notify client within 24 hours — before the insurance company tells them.
D1-2
Days 1–2: Reduction Requests OUT
Send to ALL providers within 48 hours. They don't need to be accepted — they need to be sent.
D7
Day 7: First Follow-Up
Call every lienholder — phone, not email. Confirm receipt of reduction request. Update client on disbursement timeline.
D14
Every 14 Days: Client Update
Even if nothing has changed. "Never let a client call asking about their check without having already updated them."
D30
Day 30: Flag to Attorney
If still open, flag to Pre-Lit Head Attorney with explanation of what's holding it up.
D45
Day 45: Mandatory Attorney Review
Managing Attorney determines path to closure. 90%+ of files should be closed by now.
🚨 THE PARTIAL DISBURSEMENT RULE: No client should wait months with zero funds while you resolve liens. Hold ONLY filed lien amounts + a cushion in trust. Disburse EVERYTHING else immediately. If a lien is $15,000 on a $50,000 settlement — the client gets their share of the remaining $35,000 NOW.
