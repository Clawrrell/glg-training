Why This Module Exists

Insurance verification is not a checkbox. It is a recovery investigation.

You have already learned how to retrieve records (LA-1), send Letters of Representation (LA-2), build lien packets (LA-3), and keep CasePeer accurate (LA-4). The Coverage Landscape

BI · PD · UM/UIM · MedPay — what each is and when it applies.
⚡ Why This Matters

The demand was built. The strategy was set. And the UM field in CasePeer had been blank for 45 days.

The attorney asked one question in the demand review meeting: "What does total coverage look like?" The at-fault BI limits were confirmed — $25,000. But the client had a $100,000 Excess UM policy sitting untouched on her own policy. Nobody had asked. The UM field was blank. The demand strategy had been calibrated to the wrong number for six weeks.

📊 The Four Coverage Types at a Glance

CoverageWho Carries ItWhat It DoesWhy It Matters
BI (Bodily Injury)At-fault driver's policyPays the injured party from the at-fault driver's liability policyFirst source to confirm; sets the baseline recovery amount
PD (Property Damage)At-fault driver's policyPays for vehicle damage to the injured party's carSeparate from BI; needs its own authorization process
UM/UIMClient's own policy (and household members)Pays when at-fault driver is uninsured or underinsuredCan dramatically increase total recovery — often missed
MedPayClient's own policyNo-fault medical payment coverage — pays bills immediately, regardless of who caused the accidentAvailable now — while treatment is ongoing, before any settlement

⚡ Excess vs. Reduced UM — The Math That Changes Everything

EXCESS / ADD-ON UM: The UM limit stacks ON TOP of the at-fault liability payout. If the at-fault driver pays $25K and the client has $100K Excess UM → $125K total available.

REDUCED / OFFSET UM: The UM limit only covers the GAP above the at-fault payout. $100K Reduced UM minus $25K at-fault = UM pays $75K → still $100K total, just from two sources.

Always check the dec page to confirm which type the client has. This changes every calculation.

Opening the Insurance Claims

Templates 01, 02, 62 — the right letter to the right insurer, on time, every time.
⚡ Why This Matters

The LOR went out on time. But only to the at-fault carrier.

Nobody sent Template 02 or Template 62 to the client's UM carrier. The at-fault driver had $25,000 in liability limits. The client had $200,000 in damages — and a $175,000 UM policy sitting unused. When the attorney tried to open the UM claim 60 days later, the carrier's first argument was late notice. Three weeks of back-and-forth. A demand that should have closed at policy limits settled for significantly less.

📋 The Three Intake Templates for Coverage Claims

#TemplateSent ToWhen to Send
01LOR At-FaultAt-fault driver's liability insurerAt intake — within 48 hours of paralegal instruction
02LOR UMClient's own UM carrierAt intake — simultaneously with Template 01 if UM exists
62UM Insurer On NoticeClient's own UM carrierAt intake — sent WITH Template 02; puts carrier on formal notice of potential UM claim

Key rule: Templates 02 and 62 go to the SAME UM carrier but serve different legal purposes. Template 02 opens the file and establishes representation. Template 62 formally notifies the carrier of a potential UM claim — protecting the client's rights under the policy.

⏱️ The 48-Hour LOR Deadline

Per the Legal Assistant SOP: Letters of Representation must be drafted and sent within 48 hours of paralegal instruction.

The clock starts the moment the paralegal assigns the task — not when you become available, not after email, not after the other case you're helping with. The 48-hour window is a hard standard.

⚠️ Never delay UM claim opening while waiting on at-fault confirmation. UM claims have their own notice deadlines. Open everything simultaneously at intake.

Coverage Confirmation Calls

What to confirm, how to document, and what to do when the answer is no.
⚡ Why This Matters

"Called Progressive — confirmed limits." Four words in CasePeer. Three months of phantom coverage.

The demand went out. The strategy was built around $100,000 in UM coverage. In demand review, the paralegal asked: "Is that UM Excess or Reduced?" Nobody knew. The note said nothing. The LA called Progressive again. The new rep said: "There is no UM on this policy." The first rep had been wrong. The demand had to be restructured. Two weeks lost. One case note that asked for nothing, confirmed nothing, and proved nothing.

📞 What to Confirm in Every Coverage Call

  1. Carrier confirmed in force — was coverage active on the date of loss?
  2. Policy limits by type — BI limits, PD limits, UM limits (if applicable)
  3. UM type — is the UM Excess (Add-On) or Reduced (Offset)?
  4. Dec page availability — request the declaration page on every call
  5. Claim number — get it if one has been assigned
  6. Rep's full name, title, and direct contact — not the main phone number, not "a rep told me"

📝 The Documentation Standard for a Coverage Call

Every coverage call note must pass the handoff test: could a colleague who has never touched this file read your note and know exactly what was confirmed, who confirmed it, and what happens next?

Required elements: Date and time · Rep's full name and direct contact · Specific limits confirmed by type · Excess vs. Reduced (for UM) · Dec page requested and expected timeline · Claim number · Follow-up task set in CasePeer · Coverage fields updated in CasePeer

UM/UIM Source Identification

Five sources. All five must be checked on every case. Every single time.
⚡ Why This Matters

Client Teresa Vega was a passenger in her brother Miguel's car. The case closed. Two months later — the firm found out what it missed.

The LA sent Template 01 to the at-fault carrier and Template 02 to Teresa's own UM policy. $75,000 — and the demand closed. Two months later, a new CM noticed that Miguel's mother owned the car and had a separate $250,000 UM policy through Allstate. Teresa had been a guest insured under her mother's policy the entire time. Nobody had checked the vehicle owner's policy. The firm missed a six-figure recovery source that could not be reopened after the release was signed.

📋 The Five Sources of UM Coverage — Check All Five, Every Case

  1. Client's own UM policy — UM follows the person, not the car. Even if they were in someone else's vehicle or on foot.
  2. UM policy on the vehicle client was in — if the client was a passenger, that vehicle's UM policy may cover them as a "guest."
  3. Vehicle owner's UM (when driver ≠ owner) — if the driver had the owner's permission, the owner's UM policy extends to guests in that vehicle.
  4. Resident relative's UM — spouses and relatives living in the same household are covered under each other's UM policies, even across different vehicles.
  5. Multi-vehicle stacking — some policies allow per-vehicle stacking when the named insured has multiple vehicles; always confirm with the attorney before assuming this applies.

🚨 The $100K Escalation Rule

When total available UM coverage across ALL policies exceeds $100,000 — alert Adriauna (Lead Pre-Lit) IMMEDIATELY. Not at the weekly check-in. Not after you finish the rest of the intake. Immediately.

This is a required escalation, not a suggestion. Cases with total UM above $100K require elevated strategy from the moment that threshold is confirmed.

Documentation & Escalation

What goes in CasePeer, when, and when to pick up the phone.
⚡ Why This Matters

$350,000 in UM confirmed at intake. Worked as a routine case for 11 months.

The coverage audit came six weeks before trial. Three separate UM policies. $350,000 total. The $100K escalation threshold had been met on Day 1. Nobody had alerted Adriauna. The case had been worked with a routine demand strategy — for nearly a year. At trial prep, the partners asked: "Why wasn't this escalated?" The answer was silence. Someone had confirmed the limits. The data was in CasePeer. But the escalation never happened.

📋 CasePeer Coverage Standards

  • Update coverage fields IMMEDIATELY upon confirmation — same day, not end of week
  • Request the dec page on every coverage call — verbal confirmation is a starting point, not the finish line
  • Dec page = required documentation — demand packages must be supported by the actual dec page, not a phone call note
  • Cover all fields — carrier name, policy limits, coverage type (Excess vs. Reduced), coverage status on date of loss
