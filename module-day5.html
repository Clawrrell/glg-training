Section 1

Days 1–4 Key Concepts Review

Before the assessment, let's make sure the key concepts from this week are locked in. Here's the essential summary:

📋 Day 1 — GLG Overview & Your Role
  • Gunn Law Group: personal injury firm in Atlanta, GA
  • Your job = Legal Assistant (LA) supporting the Case Manager (CM)
  • Cases move through a pipeline: Intake → Treating → Demand → Settlement → Disbursement → Closed
  • The CM drives. You keep it running. Consistent follow-up is everything.
⚖️ Day 2 — PI Case Types & CasePeer
  • Case types: Auto, Trucking, Premises Liability, Pedestrian/Bicycle, Dog Bites, Wrongful Death
  • Liability = who's at fault. Georgia: 50%+ at fault = zero recovery (O.C.G.A. § 51-12-33)
  • Claim (with insurance) ≠ Lawsuit (in court). Most cases settle at the claim stage.
  • CasePeer status accuracy is critical — wrong status = case falls through the cracks
🛡️ Day 3 — Insurance in PI
  • BI (Bodily Injury) = injuries to others. PD (Property Damage) = vehicle damage. Both separate.
  • UM = uninsured (no coverage). UIM = underinsured (not enough). Both on client's own policy.
  • MedPay = pays medical bills regardless of fault. Creates a subrogation lien at settlement.
  • Policy limits cap recovery. O.C.G.A. § 33-3-28: insurers must disclose limits within 30 days of written request.
  • NEVER let clients give recorded statements without attorney approval.
📄 Day 4 — Records, Templates & LORs
  • LOR = to the insurance company ("we represent this client" — send within 24-48 hrs)
  • LOP = to the medical provider ("treat now, paid at settlement" — creates a lien)
  • Police reports: get them, look for at-fault driver info, citations, witnesses
  • Medical records: always request records AND billing together. HIPAA auth required.
  • 14-day follow-up rule: every outstanding request gets a follow-up every 14 days
Section 2

How Everything Connects: Intake to Settlement

Every step you've learned this week connects to the next. Here's the full picture:

1
Intake
Client calls in. We evaluate the case. Client signs the fee agreement. You: Send LOR to insurance company within 24-48 hours. Open claim. Verify coverage and policy limits. Get HIPAA auth signed. Identify all providers.
2
Treating
Client is going to doctors. This is the longest phase. You: Send records requests to every provider (with HIPAA auth). Request records AND bills. Follow up every 14 days. Track MedPay/LOP providers. Monitor client's treatment — are they still going?
3
Pending Demand
Treatment complete. All records received. You: Organize all records and bills for the demand package. Confirm all providers are accounted for. CM reviews and sends the demand letter.
4
Demanded → Settlement Negotiations
Demand sent. Insurer responds with an offer. Negotiating begins. You: Track all communications. Send lien reduction letters to LOP providers. Monitor deadlines.
5
Settled → Disbursement
Settlement agreed. You: Help prepare the settlement statement. Confirm all lien amounts are final. Ensure LOP/MedPay subrogation liens are identified. Client gets paid after all liens and fees are deducted.

The case only moves when you keep pushing it forward.

Every delay in records, every missed follow-up, every wrong status in CasePeer — it adds weeks to the client's timeline and stalls their recovery. Your work directly affects the client's life.

Section 3

A Typical Day in the Life of a Legal Assistant

No two days are exactly the same, but here's what a solid day looks like as a Legal Assistant at GLG:

🌅
Morning: Check Your Task List
Open CasePeer. Review all tasks due today. What records follow-ups are due? What LOR confirmations are pending? What clients need to be called? Prioritize: urgent tasks first.
📞
Mid-Morning: Calls & Follow-Ups
Call providers on the 14-day follow-up list. Confirm claim numbers and adjuster info. Reach out to clients who haven't checked in recently — are they still treating? Log every call in CasePeer.
📬
Midday: Process Incoming Mail & Records
Check for incoming records, faxes, and emails. Upload to CasePeer immediately. Flag any records that show concerning treatment gaps or missing billing. Pass anything notable to the CM.
📋
Afternoon: New Cases & Admin
Help set up new cases: enter client info in CasePeer, send LORs, prepare records requests with HIPAA auth, order police reports. Use templates to speed everything up.
🌇
End of Day: Update & Set Tomorrow
Log all completed tasks. Set follow-up tasks for 14 days out. Update CasePeer notes. Check that all case statuses are accurate. Leave your task list clean for tomorrow.

✅ The Golden Rule

If it didn't get logged in CasePeer, it didn't happen. Every call, every follow-up, every document received — it goes in the system. This protects the client, protects the firm, and protects you.

Section 4

Common Mistakes to Avoid in Week 2

These are the most common mistakes new Legal Assistants make. Know them before you make them:

❌
Letting clients give recorded statements
✅ Fix: Tell every client on day one — all communication with insurance goes through us. Never talk to the adjuster alone.
❌
Forgetting to send the LOR within 24-48 hours
✅ Fix: Make the LOR the FIRST task after a client signs. Before anything else. The insurer can contact them until the LOR arrives.
❌
Requesting records but not billing (or vice versa)
✅ Fix: Always request BOTH in the same letter. Records + itemized billing. Every time. No exceptions.
❌
Missing a 14-day follow-up
✅ Fix: Set the follow-up task in CasePeer THE DAY you send the request. Don't trust your memory. The task is your backup.
❌
Leaving case statuses wrong or stale
✅ Fix: Check statuses regularly. If a client has stopped treating but the status still says "Treating," flag it to the CM today.
❌
Not logging calls in CasePeer
✅ Fix: Log EVERY call — even if you didn't reach anyone. "Called [provider] at 2:15pm, no answer, left voicemail." This is your paper trail.
❌
Assuming UM/UIM coverage exists without verifying
✅ Fix: Always verify our client's own policy — UM/UIM limits, MedPay, health insurance. Never assume. Always confirm.

Week 2 is about applying what you learned.

These mistakes don't come from not caring — they come from rushing. Slow down on the important steps. Fast follow-up, careful documentation, accurate status updates. That's the job.
