What is Personal Injury Law?

The Foundation: Helping Injured People

🎯 GLG's Mission: We represent people who have been hurt through no fault of their own. We fight for fair compensation so our clients can rebuild their lives.

Personal injury law exists because accidents happen — and when they do, someone is usually at fault. Our job is to prove who was negligent and get our clients the money they need to move forward.

Essential Lawyering: Problem-Solving, Not Just Law

🧠 Key Insight: "Excellent lawyering is built on interviewing, counseling, negotiation, and persuasive fact analysis — not just knowing the law." Every GLG team member uses these skills.

You don't have to be an attorney to apply these principles:

  • Interviewing: Learning the client's real situation and concerns
  • Counseling: Explaining options clearly and helping clients decide
  • Negotiation: Understanding what motivates the other side
  • Fact Analysis: Building a story that shows why our client deserves compensation

The Four Elements of Negligence

Every personal injury case requires proving four things:

  1. Duty: The defendant owed a duty to be careful
  2. Breach: They violated that duty
  3. Causation: Their violation caused the accident
  4. Damages: The plaintiff suffered real harm

If any element is missing, there's no case. All four must be proven for a successful outcome.

Client-Centered Approach

❤️ Remember: Every case decision starts with "how does this serve the client?" The legal result AND the client experience both matter.

PI clients are scared, hurt, and dealing with insurance companies for the first time. They need an attorney who fights for them AND treats them with empathy throughout the process.

Reptile Safety Rule Framework

How GLG Thinks About Cases

🦎 Reptile Theory: Jurors are hardwired to protect themselves and their community from danger. When you frame a case around violated safety rules, you activate this instinct.

We don't just argue "the defendant was careless." We argue "the defendant violated a safety rule that protects everyone — including you, the juror. The community must enforce this rule."

Safety Rules Must Be Universal

A good safety rule is:

  • Simple and clear: Anyone can understand it
  • Applicable broadly: Protects everyone, not just this plaintiff
  • Something jurors rely on: They follow this rule themselves
✅ Example: "A driver must maintain a safe following distance to prevent rear-end collisions." This isn't just about our client — it's about every person on Georgia roads.

Common GLG Safety Rules

Motor Vehicle Cases

A driver must pay attention to the road at all times
Applies to distracted driving, texting, falling asleep, impairment

Premises Liability

Property owners must maintain safe conditions for visitors
Applies to slip/falls, inadequate security, dangerous conditions

Medical Malpractice

Healthcare providers must follow accepted standards of care
Applies to misdiagnosis, surgical errors, medication mistakes

Products Liability

Manufacturers must design products that are safe for intended use
Applies to defective products, inadequate warnings, design flaws

Three Key Questions for Every Case

From the Reptile methodology:

  1. Who else could have been a victim? (Makes it universal, not personal)
  2. Why should the juror and community care? (Activates protective instinct)
  3. What shows it could happen again? (Creates urgency to enforce the rule)

If we can't answer these questions, we may not have a strong Reptile case.

Georgia Law & Procedures

Georgia-Specific Requirements

Every state has different laws. Here are key Georgia statutes that affect our cases:

O.C.G.A. § 51-12-1
Statute of limitations for personal injury cases — generally 2 years from date of injury
O.C.G.A. § 51-11-7
Comparative negligence — plaintiff's fault reduces recovery proportionally
O.C.G.A. § 40-6-391
Georgia's DUI statute — creates presumptions about impairment
O.C.G.A. § 51-12-5.1
Georgia's seat belt defense limitation
O.C.G.A. § 40-6-270
Following too closely statute — useful in rear-end cases

Georgia Insurance Requirements

🛡️ Georgia Minimum Liability Insurance: $25,000 per person / $50,000 per accident for bodily injury, $25,000 for property damage (25/50/25)

This is often inadequate for serious injuries. Part of our job is identifying all available insurance coverage:

  • Primary liability: At-fault driver's insurance
  • UM/UIM: Client's underinsured/uninsured motorist coverage
  • MedPay/PIP: Immediate medical coverage regardless of fault
  • Umbrella policies: Additional coverage beyond standard limits

Comparative Negligence in Georgia

Georgia follows the "modified comparative negligence" rule:

  • If plaintiff is 0-49% at fault → recovers damages reduced by their fault percentage
  • If plaintiff is 50% or more at fault → recovers nothing
⚖️ Example: Client is 20% at fault in a rear-end crash. Total damages are $100,000. Client recovers $80,000 ($100,000 - 20%).

This is why it's crucial to minimize our client's fault and maximize the defendant's in every case story.

Inquiring Mode vs. Persuasion Mode

From Essential Lawyering — every client conversation needs both:

  • Inquiring Mode: "Tell me what happened." "What's your biggest concern?" "How has this affected you?"
  • Persuasion Mode: "Here's what we recommend." "Based on similar cases..." "Your options are..."
⚠️ Key Mistake: Jumping to persuasion mode too quickly. Always understand the client's real concerns before explaining solutions.
