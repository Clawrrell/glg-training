Case Type 1 of 9
Motor Vehicle Accidents
🚗
Car Crashes & Vehicle Collisions

Common Scenarios

  • Rear-end collisions (most common GLG case type)
  • Intersection crashes (T-bone, failure to yield)
  • Head-on collisions
  • Hit and run accidents
  • Distracted driving (texting, phone use)
  • Impaired driving (DUI/DWI)
Safety Rule
Every driver must follow traffic laws and operate their vehicle with reasonable care to protect all road users

Case Evaluation Checklist

  • Who violated the safety rule? (speeding, following too close, running light)
  • What evidence exists? (police report, photos, witnesses, surveillance)
  • Is liability clear or disputed?
  • What are client's injuries and treatment status?
  • Insurance coverage available? (liability limits, UM/UIM)
  • Any comparative negligence issues?

CasePeer Workflow

New Intake → Initial Case Review
Signed → Send LORs & Obtain Records
Treating → Monitor Client Progress
Records Received → Attorney Review
Demand Sent → Negotiation Phase
Settlement Reached → Distribution

Client Communication Focus

Motor vehicle clients are often dealing with their first accident. Key communication points: explain the process clearly, set realistic timelines, monitor treatment compliance, and prepare them for insurance tactics.

Case Type 2 of 9
Commercial Trucking Accidents
🚛
18-Wheeler & Commercial Vehicle Crashes

Common Scenarios

  • 18-wheeler vs. passenger car collisions
  • Jackknife accidents
  • Delivery truck crashes (FedEx, UPS, Amazon)
  • Construction vehicle accidents
  • Bus accidents (school bus, MARTA)
  • Overloaded or improperly secured cargo
Safety Rule
Commercial drivers must follow federal safety regulations and operate with extra care due to their vehicle's size and potential for catastrophic harm

Case Evaluation Checklist

  • Driver logbook compliance? (Hours of Service violations)
  • Vehicle maintenance records? (brake failures, tire issues)
  • Driver qualification? (CDL status, medical certification)
  • Company safety record and policies?
  • Insurance coverage? (Often $1M+ commercial policies)
  • Multiple defendants? (driver, trucking company, cargo owner)

Special Considerations

Evidence preservation is critical: Trucking companies have sophisticated legal teams and preserve evidence quickly. GLG must act fast to secure driver logs, maintenance records, and electronic data.

Federal regulations apply: FMCSA rules create specific safety standards that strengthen safety rule arguments.

Client Communication Focus

Trucking accidents often involve catastrophic injuries. Clients need extensive medical care coordination and realistic timeline expectations. These cases take longer but often have higher values due to commercial insurance.

Case Type 3 of 9
Motorcycle Accidents
🏍️
Motorcycle vs. Vehicle Collisions

Common Scenarios

  • Left-turn accidents (car turns in front of motorcycle)
  • "Didn't see the motorcycle" crashes
  • Lane-splitting incidents
  • Rear-end collisions
  • Road hazard crashes (debris, potholes)
  • Hit and run accidents
Safety Rule
All drivers must actively look for motorcycles and share the road safely — motorcyclists have the same rights as any other vehicle operator

Case Evaluation Checklist

  • Visibility factors? (time of day, weather, motorcycle lighting)
  • Speed and lane position of both vehicles?
  • Right of way analysis?
  • Helmet use? (affects damages, not liability in Georgia)
  • Motorcycle experience/licensing?
  • Severity of injuries? (often more severe due to lack of protection)

Special Challenges

Bias against motorcyclists: Insurance adjusters and juries often assume motorcyclists are reckless. GLG must counter this with evidence of the car driver's violation of safety rules.

Severe injuries common: Road rash, fractures, head trauma, and permanent disability are frequent outcomes requiring extensive medical documentation.

Client Communication Focus

Motorcycle clients often face long recovery periods and may be passionate about riding. Show empathy for their lifestyle while focusing on the other driver's responsibility to see and avoid them.

Case Type 4 of 9
Slip and Fall / Premises Liability
🚶
Premises Liability & Property Accidents

Common Scenarios

  • Wet floor slips (grocery stores, restaurants)
  • Uneven surfaces, cracked sidewalks
  • Inadequate lighting causing falls
  • Debris or merchandise in walkways
  • Defective stairs or handrails
  • Ice/snow accumulation
  • Swimming pool accidents
Safety Rule
Property owners must maintain safe conditions for all visitors and warn of known hazards that could cause serious injury

Case Evaluation Checklist

  • Was the hazard visible and obvious?
  • How long did the condition exist?
  • Did the property owner know or should have known?
  • Were there warnings posted?
  • What caused the fall? (constructive vs. actual notice)
  • Client's attention/awareness at time of fall?

Georgia Law Specifics

Notice requirement: Must prove owner had actual or constructive notice of the dangerous condition.

Open and obvious doctrine: Property owner may not be liable for hazards that are open and obvious to visitors.

Comparative negligence applies: Client's failure to pay attention can reduce recovery.

Evidence Collection Priority

Photos of scene, witness statements, incident reports, surveillance video, maintenance logs, and weather conditions are all critical and may disappear quickly.

Client Communication Focus

Slip and fall clients often feel embarrassed about their accident. Reassure them that property owners have a legal duty to maintain safe premises. Focus on the violation of that duty.

Case Type 5 of 9
Medical Malpractice
⚕️
Healthcare Provider Negligence

Common Scenarios

  • Misdiagnosis or delayed diagnosis
  • Surgical errors
  • Medication mistakes
  • Birth injuries
  • Hospital infections
  • Failure to obtain informed consent
  • Emergency room negligence
Safety Rule
Healthcare providers must follow accepted standards of medical care to protect every patient who entrusts them with their health and safety

Case Evaluation Checklist

  • What is the standard of care that should have been followed?
  • How did the provider deviate from that standard?
  • Did the deviation cause the patient's injury?
  • What would have happened with proper care?
  • Expert witness needed to establish standard?
  • Damages calculation including future medical needs?

Georgia Medical Malpractice Requirements

Expert affidavit required: Must file expert affidavit within 120 days of complaint.

Statute of limitations: Generally 2 years from discovery of injury.

Damage caps: Non-economic damages capped at $350,000 (with exceptions).

Special Considerations

High cost and complexity: Medical records review, multiple experts, and extended litigation make these cases expensive to pursue.

Informed consent: Different standard than negligence — must prove patient would have refused treatment if properly informed.

Client Communication Focus

Medical malpractice clients have lost trust in the healthcare system. Provide clear explanations of the legal process and realistic timelines. These cases take 2-4 years to resolve.

Case Type 6 of 9
Dog Bite Cases
🐕
Dog Attacks & Animal Injuries

Common Scenarios

  • Dog bites on public property
  • Attacks on private property
  • Children injured by family dogs
  • Delivery worker/postal worker attacks
  • Dog park incidents
  • Leash law violations
Safety Rule
Dog owners must control their animals and prevent them from harming others, especially children and visitors who cannot protect themselves

Georgia Dog Bite Law

O.C.G.A. § 51-2-7: "Vicious propensity" or violation of leash law required for liability.

Not strict liability: Unlike some states, Georgia requires proof of owner's knowledge of dog's vicious nature or violation of local ordinance.

Leash law violations: Create presumption of negligence in many Georgia municipalities.

Case Evaluation Checklist

  • Prior incidents or aggressive behavior by this dog?
  • Leash law violation? (check local ordinances)
  • Where did attack occur? (affects liability)
  • What provoked the attack, if anything?
  • Age of victim? (children given more protection)
  • Homeowner's/renter's insurance coverage?

Special Challenges

Insurance coverage: Many policies exclude dog bites or have breed restrictions.

Emotional trauma: Especially in children, psychological counseling may be needed.

Scarring and disfigurement: Require plastic surgery consultations and long-term treatment plans.

Client Communication Focus

Dog bite victims, especially children and parents, are traumatized by the attack. Focus on the owner's responsibility to control their animal and prevent exactly this type of community danger.

Case Type 7 of 9
Wrongful Death
💀
Fatal Accident Cases

Common Scenarios

  • Fatal car accidents
  • Trucking accident deaths
  • Medical malpractice deaths
  • Workplace fatalities
  • Defective product deaths
  • Premises liability deaths
Safety Rule
Same safety rule as underlying case — but the violation was so severe it took someone's life, leaving family members to suffer the ultimate consequence

Georgia Wrongful Death Law

O.C.G.A. § 51-4-1 et seq.: Spouse and children are preferred beneficiaries.

Full value of life: Includes both economic and intangible value.

Estate claim separate: Pain/suffering before death goes to estate.

Priority of claimants: Spouse/children → parents → estate

Case Evaluation Checklist

  • Who are the statutory beneficiaries?
  • Deceased's age, health, and life expectancy?
  • Income, earning capacity, and career trajectory?
  • Family relationships and dependencies?
  • Pain and suffering before death? (estate claim)
  • Available insurance coverage for wrongful death claim?

Special Considerations

Economic expert needed: Calculate lost future earnings and household services.

Day in the life evidence: Document deceased's relationships and contributions to family.

Immediate investigation critical: Evidence deteriorates quickly in fatal cases.

Client Communication Focus

Wrongful death families are grieving and may not be ready to discuss legal matters immediately. Provide compassionate support while preserving evidence. Explain that the legal process helps prevent similar tragedies.

Case Type 8 of 9
Uber/Lyft Accidents
🚗
Rideshare Vehicle Accidents

Common Scenarios

  • Passenger injured in rideshare vehicle
  • Rideshare driver causes crash injuring others
  • Another driver hits rideshare vehicle
  • Pedestrian struck by rideshare vehicle
  • Accidents during pickup/dropoff
Safety Rule
Rideshare drivers must drive safely and follow all traffic laws while carrying passengers who have entrusted them with their safety

Complex Insurance Coverage

Driver's personal insurance: Often excludes commercial activity

Uber/Lyft coverage depends on driver status:

App off → Driver's personal insurance only
App on, no ride → Limited Uber/Lyft coverage
Ride accepted → Full Uber/Lyft coverage ($1M+)

Case Evaluation Checklist

  • Driver's status at time of accident?
  • Was driver following Uber/Lyft policies?
  • Driver's qualification and background check?
  • Vehicle inspection and maintenance records?
  • App data and GPS tracking available?
  • Which insurance policy applies and limits?

Special Challenges

Technology evidence: App data, GPS routes, and driver ratings can be crucial but may require litigation to obtain.

Corporate defendants: Uber/Lyft argue they are technology platforms, not transportation companies.

Client Communication Focus

Rideshare accident clients often don't understand the complex insurance issues. Explain that coverage depends on driver status and that GLG will navigate the insurance maze for them.

Case Type 9 of 9
Premises Liability (Beyond Slip & Fall)
🏢
Property-Related Injuries

Common Scenarios

  • Inadequate security (assaults, robberies)
  • Swimming pool drownings
  • Balcony/deck collapses
  • Falling objects (construction sites)
  • Elevator/escalator malfunctions
  • Fire safety violations
  • Toxic exposure on property
Safety Rule
Property owners must maintain safe conditions and provide adequate security to protect all visitors from foreseeable harm

Case Evaluation Checklist

  • What specific hazard or security failure occurred?
  • Was the condition or risk foreseeable?
  • Did owner/manager know or should have known?
  • What safety measures should have been in place?
  • Prior similar incidents at this location?
  • Building code or safety violations?

Special Evidence Needs

Inadequate security cases: Crime statistics, prior incidents, security assessments, lighting studies

Structural failures: Engineering reports, building permits, inspection records

Code violations: Municipal records, fire department reports, OSHA citations

Georgia Premises Liability Categories

Invitee: Business visitor — highest duty of care

Licensee: Social guest — duty to warn of known hazards

Trespasser: Unauthorized presence — limited duty except to children

Client Communication Focus

Premises liability clients often feel the property owner "should have known better." Focus on the owner's duty to maintain safe conditions for everyone who might be on the property.
