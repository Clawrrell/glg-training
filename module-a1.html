Welcome to Gunn Law Group

What We Do

Gunn Law Group is a personal injury law firm based in Atlanta, Georgia. We represent people who have been hurt through no fault of their own — in car accidents, trucking crashes, slip and falls, and more.

Our job is to make it right. We fight for fair compensation so our clients can rebuild their lives. We are on the injured person's side — always.

Our Mission

🎯 Fight for fair compensation for every injured client — with relentless advocacy, systems that work, and a team that holds the line.

What Makes Us Different

  • Systems: We don't rely on memory. Every step has a process, template, and deadline. When the system works, clients win.
  • Accountability: We verify. We follow up. We escalate when needed. No one drops the ball without it being caught.
  • Client Communication: Our clients are scared, hurt, and waiting. We call them before they call us. We treat them like family.

Your Role Matters

💡 There are no unimportant jobs here. Every position — from intake to distribution — directly affects whether a client gets what they deserve. You are part of that chain.

When records come in late, demands go out late. When demands go out late, settlements are delayed. When settlements are delayed, clients suffer. Your work has real consequences for real people.

The Case Lifecycle — Plain English

1
Someone gets hurt — accident, slip & fall, or other incident
2
They call us — Intake screens the case and signs the client
3
We send LORs — Notify insurance we represent the client
4
Client treats — We monitor treatment and maintain contact
5
We get records — Request medical records and bills
6
We send a demand — Attorney sends demand package to insurance
7
Insurance pays or we sue — Negotiate or file a lawsuit
8
Client gets their money — Settlement distributed within 14 days

Your Position in the Machine

⚙️ Every department moves the case forward. Think of it as an assembly line. If one station stops, the whole line backs up. Know your station. Own your station.

Whether you're in intake, pre-lit, records retrieval, negotiations, or distribution — your output is someone else's input. Do your job right, on time, and the client wins faster.

Section 3 of 6
What We Expect

Client Communication Standards

📞
Pre-Litigation clients: Contact every 14 days — no exceptions
Litigation clients: Contact every 30 days
Missed calls: Return within 24 hours

Don't wait for clients to call you. Proactive communication = trust. Trust = cooperation. Cooperation = faster settlements.

CasePeer — The Rule

💻 If it's not documented in CasePeer, it didn't happen. A conversation, a callback, a status update — if it's not logged, it doesn't exist as far as this firm is concerned.

This protects you, the firm, and the client. Document everything. Every call. Every email. Every action taken on a file.

Time Clock & Attendance

  • 4 punches per day: Clock in → Clock out for lunch → Clock in from lunch → Clock out EOD
  • 7-minute grace period on tardiness — beyond that, it's documented
  • 60-minute lunch maximum — plan accordingly
  • Attendance issues follow the progressive discipline process
  • If you're going to be late or absent, notify your CM before your shift starts

Professional Conduct

  • Treat every client like they're a family member in crisis — because they are
  • Treat every colleague with respect — we win as a team
  • Dress and communicate professionally at all times
  • Client information stays inside these walls — always

Chain of Command

📋
Issue with a case? → Your CM
Issue with your CM? → Department Head
HR or firm policy question? → Managing Attorney's office
When in doubt: Ask up — never guess

What's Not Tolerated
🚫 These are not "frowned upon" behaviors. These are fireable offenses or triggers for immediate disciplinary action. Read every one.
📵
Zero communication with clients — Going days or weeks without contacting your clients is abandonment. It harms their case and reflects on the firm.
📱
Ignoring callbacks — Every missed call must be returned within 24 hours. No exceptions. Log every attempt.
📂
Cases sitting in wrong status — CasePeer status must reflect reality at all times. A case in "Treating" for 8 months with no movement is a failure.
⏰
Missing deadlines without escalating — If you can't hit a deadline, say so before it passes. Silence is not acceptable. Escalate early.
🫵
Blaming others when you didn't verify — "I thought they handled it" is not a defense. Delegation is not abdication. If you assigned it, you're responsible for confirming it was done.
🔐
Sharing client information outside the firm — This is a HIPAA and confidentiality violation. It can end your career in this industry. It will end your career here immediately.
💬 If you're struggling, overwhelmed, or unsure — talk to your CM before something falls through the cracks. We would rather help you course-correct than have to discipline you.
Section 5 of 6
How You're Measured

Monthly Scorecard

Every employee receives a monthly performance rating. Your rating directly affects your compensation and advancement. Here's what each grade means:

A
Exceeds Expectations Merit raise + 100% bonus payout. This is the standard we want everyone striving for.
B
Meets Expectations Standard raise + 75% bonus payout. Solid performance, room to grow.
C
Below Expectations COLA raise only + 50% bonus. A signal that improvement is needed.
D
Unsatisfactory No raise + 0% bonus + Performance Improvement Plan (PIP). Continued D rating leads to termination.

Performance Reviews & Coaching

Scorecards are reviewed monthly with your CM. This isn't just a number — it's a conversation about what's working and what needs to change. We want to see you succeed.

Progressive Discipline

1️⃣
Coaching — Informal conversation, guidance, correction
2️⃣
Verbal Warning — Documented discussion, specific improvement required
3️⃣
Written Warning — Formal written notice in your HR file
4️⃣
Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) — Structured plan with measurable goals and timeline
5️⃣
Termination — Employment ends if PIP goals are not met
✅ Note: Verbal warnings trigger after 3 tardies per pay period. The policy scales with severity and frequency. Coaching is always the first step — we want to help you get it right.
