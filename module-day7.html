Section 1

Coverage Verification — The First Economic Analysis

Coverage verification is not paperwork — it's economic analysis. Before you can evaluate settlement strategy, set client expectations, or frame a PI strategy safety argument, you must know the available money. This is your first and most critical financial task on every case.

💰 Why Coverage Drives Everything

  • Settlement Strategy: $25K limits = policy limits demand. $500K limits = build the damages case
  • Client Expectations: Never let a client believe they're getting $100K when only $25K exists
  • Case Investment: High limits justify expensive medical treatment. Low limits don't
  • Timeline Planning: Known limits enable time-limited demands that create settlement pressure
  • UM/UIM Strategy: If at-fault limits are exhausted, client's own UM/UIM coverage becomes primary

📖 Georgia Minimum Coverage Requirements

25/50/25

$25,000 per person for Bodily Injury
$50,000 per accident for Bodily Injury
$25,000 per accident for Property Damage

Many Georgia drivers carry only state minimums. Assume minimums until you verify otherwise.

Every dollar of available coverage is money for the client.

Missing coverage at intake means missing money at settlement. Your job is to find every source of recovery.

Section 2

O.C.G.A. § 33-3-28 — Policy Limits Disclosure

This Georgia statute is a powerful tool for discovering policy limits. Insurance companies cannot hide behind "we don't disclose limits" — the law requires it.

O.C.G.A. § 33-3-28

Required Disclosure

Upon written request by or on behalf of a claimant, every insurer must disclose the policy limits of liability insurance coverage within 30 days of receipt.

✅ What Triggers the Law
  • Written request (not verbal)
  • From attorney or authorized agent
  • Specifically requesting "policy limits"
  • To the correct insurer
  • 30-day clock starts when they receive it
❌ What Doesn't Count
  • Phone calls asking for limits
  • General LOR without limits language
  • Request from client directly
  • Request to wrong department
  • Vague "coverage information" requests
Written Limits Request Language "Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 33-3-28, please provide the policy declaration pages and all applicable limits of liability coverage for your insured, [At-Fault Driver Name], including bodily injury, property damage, uninsured/underinsured motorist, umbrella, and any other applicable coverages in effect on [Date of Accident] for the motor vehicle accident involving our client, [Client Name]. This written request triggers the thirty (30) day disclosure requirement under Georgia law."

🎯 Strategic Use of the 30-Day Rule

  • Early Settlement Leverage: If they won't provide limits quickly, use their delay in settlement negotiations
  • Bad Faith Foundation: Failure to comply with § 33-3-28 can be evidence of bad faith at trial
  • Time-Limited Demands: Once you know limits, you can create urgency with policy limits demands
  • Client Counseling: Can't properly advise client on settlement offers without knowing available money

⚠️ Track the Deadline in CasePeer

The day you send the limits request, create a task 30 days out: "Limits response due — O.C.G.A. § 33-3-28" If no response, that's attorney work immediately.

Section 3

Opening Claims — The Professional Approach

How you open a claim sets the tone for the entire case relationship. Insurance adjusters work with attorneys all day — they know the difference between professional and amateur.

📋 Before You Call

  • Client's full name, DOB, and address
  • Date, time, and location of accident
  • At-fault driver's name and policy number (if available)
  • Brief, factual description of accident
  • Your name, title, and firm information
  • Signed LOR (or ready to send immediately)
Professional Opening Call Script "Good [morning/afternoon], my name is [Your Name] calling from the Gunn Law Group in Atlanta, Georgia. We represent [Client Name, DOB] in connection with a motor vehicle accident that occurred on [Date] at approximately [Time] in [Location] involving your insured, [At-Fault Driver Name].

I need to open a third-party liability claim. Can you first confirm that your insured had active coverage on [Date of Accident]?

[After confirmation]: Thank you. What is the claim number, and who will be the assigned adjuster? I'll be sending our letter of representation and a formal request for policy limits information today."

🚫 What Adjusters Will Try to Get

They AskYou Say
"Can I get a recorded statement from your client?""No. All communication with our client goes through our office."
"Can you give me more details about your client's injuries?""That information will be provided through the appropriate discovery process."
"When can we get medical records?""We'll provide relevant medical information as part of our demand presentation."
"What damages are you claiming?""We're still evaluating all damages. This call is to open the claim and verify coverage."

🛑 What If They Deny Coverage?

If the insurer says "no coverage" or "policy lapsed," don't argue on the phone. Get details:

  • When did the policy allegedly lapse?
  • Was there another policy in effect?
  • Will they send written denial with explanation?
  • Who made this determination?

Then immediately flag to the attorney. Coverage denials are legal issues requiring attorney review.

✅ After the Call — Same Day Actions

  • Log claim number, adjuster name/phone in CasePeer
  • Send LOR via email and certified mail
  • Send written limits request citing O.C.G.A. § 33-3-28
  • Create 30-day follow-up task for limits response
  • Email the CM and attorney: "Claim opened, coverage confirmed, limits requested"
Section 4

Multiple Coverage Sources — Maximizing Recovery

Most PI cases involve multiple sources of coverage. Your job is to identify every possible source of recovery — because every missed coverage source is money the client doesn't get.

🔄 Common Multi-Coverage Scenarios

1
At-Fault BI + Client's UIM Coverage
At-fault driver has $25K BI. Client's damages are $75K. We get $25K from at-fault BI, then pursue $50K from client's UIM policy. UIM is designed for exactly this situation.
2
MedPay Coordination
Client's MedPay pays medical bills upfront (no fault determination needed). At settlement, MedPay insurer has subrogation right — they want reimbursement from the BI recovery.
3
Health Insurance Subrogation
Health insurance pays medical bills, then seeks reimbursement from PI settlement. Some policies have full subrogation rights, others are reduced by attorney fees. Varies by policy.
4
UM/UIM Stacking
If client has multiple vehicles with UM/UIM coverage, Georgia law may allow "stacking" the limits. Two cars with $50K UM each = potentially $100K total UM coverage.
5
Multiple At-Fault Parties
Some accidents involve multiple defendants (two drivers, or driver + premises liability). Each has separate insurance, separate limits, separate claims to manage.

✅ Complete Coverage Maximization Checklist

  • At-fault driver's BI limits and PD coverage
  • Client's UM/UIM coverage and limits
  • Client's MedPay coverage and limits
  • Client's health insurance (company, policy #, subrogation rights)
  • Any umbrella or excess policies (at-fault driver)
  • Other vehicles in client's household (stacking opportunity)
  • Workers' compensation if accident occurred during work
  • Any commercial coverage (if at-fault driver was working)

⚠️ Subrogation Planning

MedPay and health insurance create subrogation liens that must be satisfied at settlement. Track these from day one:

  • How much has MedPay paid so far?
  • How much has health insurance paid?
  • What are the subrogation terms in each policy?
  • Will the liens be reduced by attorney fees?

The attorney handles lien negotiations, but you need to know they exist and track the amounts.

Think like a financial investigator.

Every insurance policy is potential money for the client. Run the full checklist on every case. Missing coverage = missing recovery.

Section 5

CasePeer Documentation — Building the System

CasePeer is the central nervous system for coverage information. Every claim number, every policy limit, every subrogation amount must be logged systematically so the attorney and CM can find it instantly.

📝 Where to Log Coverage Information

Information TypeCasePeer LocationNotes
At-fault driver's insurance companyAdverse Party → Insurance InfoInclude phone number and claim reporting line
Policy limits (when received)Adverse Party → Policy LimitsUpdate immediately when received from § 33-3-28 request
Claim numbersCase Notes + Adverse PartyBI claim #, PD claim # (often different numbers)
Assigned adjuster infoContacts → Add AdjusterDirect phone, email, supervisor name
Client's UM/UIM coverageInsured Party → Insurance InfoInclude limits and any stacking opportunities
MedPay coverage/paymentsCase Notes + Medical → PIP/MedPayTrack payments made and remaining limits
Health insurance subrogationCase Notes + Medical → Health InsuranceCompany, policy #, amounts paid, lien contact

🎯 Critical CasePeer Tasks to Create

  • Day of limits request: "Limits response due — O.C.G.A. § 33-3-28" (30 days out)
  • If no limits received: "Follow up on overdue limits request" (flag to attorney)
  • MedPay exhaustion: "MedPay approaching limit — $X remaining"
  • UIM evaluation: "Evaluate UIM claim if BI limits inadequate"
  • Multiple policies: "Confirm stacking rights for UM/UIM coverage"
1
Log immediately after opening call
Claim number, adjuster contact, coverage confirmation. Don't wait — do it while the information is fresh.
2
Update when limits are received
Policy declaration pages go in Documents section. Limits go in Adverse Party fields. Mark the § 33-3-28 task complete.
3
Track subrogation amounts monthly
MedPay and health insurance amounts change as treatment continues. Update balances monthly to avoid surprises at settlement.
4
Generate coverage summary for attorney
When limits are known, create a one-page coverage summary: all available money sources and current balances. This drives settlement strategy.

⚠️ Coverage Information is Time-Sensitive

Policy limits can change if the at-fault driver has other claims against the same policy. MedPay balances decrease as bills are paid. Health insurance subrogation amounts increase with continued treatment. Keep this information current.

Good documentation = maximum recovery.

When settlement time comes, every piece of coverage information must be instantly accessible. That's how you protect the client's financial interests.
