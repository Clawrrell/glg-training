Why Records Drive Case Value

📋 Competency Gate: Documentation Proficiency

This module maps to your 30-Day Competency Gate. Passing confirms you can prepare LORs, request medical records, use firm templates correctly, and follow the 14-day follow-up rule.

Day 30 Gate CM · LA 80% Required ~25% Caseload Gate

🏥 The GLG Standard on Records

Records quality determines case value. Insurance companies pay based on what's documented, not what the client says happened. No documentation = no case value. This is why records retrieval is the foundation of every PI case.

🏥 What Records Actually Do

  • Document the injuries — Medical records prove what happened to the client's body
  • Establish causation — Timeline from accident to treatment shows the connection
  • Quantify damages — Bills provide the economic foundation for the demand
  • Support credibility — Consistent treatment records back up the client's account
  • Counter defense arguments — Complete records prevent "pre-existing condition" claims

✅ The GLG Standard

At GLG, we don't just collect records — we build a complete medical story. Every provider, every visit, every bill. This thoroughness is what separates a $5,000 case from a $50,000 case with the same injuries.

System Principle

The records process must work without Harrell. If records retrieval depends on any individual person remembering to follow up, the system has failed. The 14-day follow-up rule and CasePeer tracking create the "franchise prototype" for records retrieval.

⚠️ What Happens Without Complete Records

Missing records = reduced case value. Insurance adjusters use gaps in the medical record to argue that injuries weren't serious, treatment wasn't necessary, or the client recovered sooner than claimed. Every missing provider is money left on the table.

Records don't happen by accident. They happen by system.

Section 2

Letter of Representation (LOR) — The Protection System

📄 What an LOR Does

  • Legal notice — Formally notifies the insurer that GLG represents the client
  • Communication control — All insurer contact must go through us after LOR receipt
  • Client protection — Prevents adjusters from getting recorded statements or quick settlements
  • Professional standard — Shows we operate by the rules from day one

Implementation Intention

"After I complete the intake checklist in CasePeer, I will send the LOR before closing the case tab." This habit stack anchors LOR sending to the intake process that already exists, making it automatic.

1
Within 24-48 hours of signing
As soon as the client signs the retainer, the LOR must go out. Every day without an LOR is a day the insurer can contact the client directly and potentially damage the case.
2
Send to ALL relevant insurers
At-fault driver's liability insurer, client's UM/UIM carrier, MedPay carrier if applicable. Don't miss any — each one can legally contact the client until they receive an LOR.
3
Use CasePeer LOR Template
GLG has standardized LOR templates in CasePeer. Navigate to the case → Documents → Templates and select the appropriate LOR template: use the "LOR — Liability" template for liability carriers and the "LOR — UM/UIM" template for UM/UIM carriers.
4
Confirm receipt and log in CasePeer
Follow up within 5-7 days to confirm the insurer received and acknowledged the LOR. Document the confirmation date and contact person in CasePeer notes.

Client-Centered Principle

The LOR protects the client experience. Without it, adjusters pressure clients into recorded statements, lowball offers, and quick settlements that don't serve their interests. The LOR is the first step in creating a professional, client-centered process.

🚨 What Happens Without an LOR

Insurance adjusters will immediately contact unrepresented clients to get recorded statements (often within 24-48 hours of the accident). These statements are used to minimize claims later. Once a client gives a recorded statement without representation, it becomes much harder to achieve full case value.

Section 3

Letter of Protection (LOP) — Treatment Access Strategy

🏥 What LOPs Enable

  • Treatment without upfront payment — Clients get care they can't afford to pay for immediately
  • Case development — More treatment = more documentation = higher case value
  • Provider relationships — LOPs help us build networks with treatment providers
  • Client care — Clients actually heal while the case develops

Problem-Solving Framework

LOPs solve the client's real problem. Clients don't just want money — they want to get better and return to their normal life. LOPs make treatment possible, which serves both the legal case and the client's actual needs.

💰
The LOP creates a medical lien
When a provider treats under an LOP, they gain a legal right to be paid from settlement proceeds. This lien must be tracked and satisfied at disbursement.
📋
Both client and firm must sign
The LOP is a three-party agreement: client (receives treatment), provider (gives treatment on credit), law firm (guarantees payment from settlement).
🎯
Common with these providers
Chiropractors, orthopedic surgeons, pain management doctors, imaging centers, physical therapy clinics. Emergency rooms typically don't work on LOPs.
💸
Negotiate liens at settlement
LOP providers often charge higher rates than insurance-negotiated rates. At settlement, we negotiate lien reductions to maximize the client's net recovery.

Process Documentation

LOP management must be systematized. Track every LOP provider in CasePeer, monitor outstanding balances, and build lien reduction into the standard settlement process. This prevents surprises at disbursement.

⚠️ LOP vs. LOR — Don't Confuse Them

LOR = Letter of Representation (sent to insurance companies) | LOP = Letter of Protection (sent to medical providers). Different recipients, different purposes. Both are critical early steps in case management.

✅ Best Practice: The Treatment Network

GLG maintains relationships with providers who regularly work on LOPs. This ensures our clients get quality treatment and we get complete records. Building this network is part of the firm's long-term strategy.

Section 4

The Records Retrieval System

Franchise Prototype

The records process must work consistently for any staff member. Following this system ensures no provider gets missed, no follow-up gets forgotten, and every case develops the medical record it needs for maximum value.

1
Get signed HIPAA authorization
HIPAA compliance requires specific written authorization before providers can release records. Use GLG's standard authorization form. One authorization typically covers all providers, but some require separate forms.
2
Identify EVERY provider
Emergency room, urgent care, primary care doctor, specialists, chiropractors, physical therapists, imaging centers, pharmacies. Ask: "Who else did you see for this accident?" Don't assume you got them all in the first interview.
3
Request records AND bills together
Always request both medical records and itemized billing statements. Records document the injuries; bills document the economic damages. Both are required for the demand package.
4
Use CasePeer Records Request Template
GLG has standardized records request templates in CasePeer. Navigate to the case → Documents → Templates and select the "Medical Records Request" template. Fill in provider name, client details, and accident date.
5
Log and set 14-day follow-up
Document the request date in CasePeer and immediately set a 14-day follow-up task. This is non-negotiable — every outstanding request gets tracked and followed up every 14 days.

The Never Miss Twice Rule

One missed follow-up is a mistake. Two in a row is a bad habit forming. The 14-day rule prevents records requests from falling through the cracks and cases from stalling.

🚨 The 14-Day Follow-Up Rule

Every outstanding records request gets a follow-up every 14 days. No exceptions. Log every follow-up call with date, time, and contact person. After three follow-ups with no response, escalate to the CM for more aggressive action.

📋 What to Include in Every Records Request

  • Signed HIPAA authorization form
  • Client name, DOB, and SSN
  • Accident date and date range of treatment
  • Request for both records AND itemized billing
  • GLG firm letterhead and contact information
  • Instructions for delivery (mail, fax, secure email)

✅ When Records Arrive

Upload to CasePeer immediately. Review for completeness — are any visit dates missing? Any providers mentioned that we haven't requested from? Any treatment gaps that need explanation? Flag issues to the CM right away.

Section 5

CasePeer Templates & The GLG Way

The GLG Way

Templates are how we systematize excellence. Every GLG letter and form should look professional, be legally compliant, and follow the same format. Templates eliminate variability and ensure consistency across all staff members.

📋 GLG CasePeer Template Library

  • LOR — Liability — Letter of Representation for at-fault driver's liability carrier
  • LOR — UM/UIM — Letter of Representation for client's UM/UIM carrier
  • Medical Records Request — Records request with HIPAA authorization
  • Records Follow-Up — Follow-up letter for outstanding records
  • Letter of Protection — Letter of Protection for medical providers
  • Lien Reduction — Lien reduction negotiation letter
📂
Access templates in CasePeer
Go to case → Documents → Templates. Select the appropriate template by name or number. Templates are organized by function and numbered for easy reference.
✏️
Fill in variable fields
Client name, accident date, provider name, insurance information — complete all bracketed fields. Never send a template with blank fields or [PLACEHOLDER] text.
👁️
Review before sending
Double-check addresses, spelling, dates, and client details. A professional letter with a typo looks unprofessional. Take 30 seconds to review every template.
📤
Send and document
Print, mail, or email as appropriate. Upload a copy to CasePeer and log the sending date. Set follow-up tasks as needed.

Client-Centered Standard

Every communication represents GLG's professional brand. Templates ensure that whether Harrell sends a letter or a new assistant does, the client and providers receive the same high-quality, professional communication.

⚠️ Template Rules

Don't modify templates without approval. If you think a template needs changes, bring it to the CM or Harrell. Templates are carefully designed for legal compliance and professional standards — ad hoc changes can create problems.

✅ The System Creates Excellence

Following the template system means every letter goes out on GLG letterhead, with proper legal language, complete information, and professional formatting. This systematic approach builds credibility with insurers, providers, and clients.

Templates aren't shortcuts. They're standards.

Every template represents GLG's commitment to doing things the right way, every time.
