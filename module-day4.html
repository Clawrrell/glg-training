Why Records Drive Case Value

The GLG Standard on Records

📋 Records quality determines case value. Insurance companies pay based on what's documented, not what the client says happened. No documentation = no case value. This is why records retrieval is the foundation of every PI case.

At GLG, we don't just collect records — we build a complete medical story. Every provider, every visit, every bill. This thoroughness is what separates a $5,000 case from a $50,000 case with the same injuries.

What Records Actually Do

📄 Document Injuries

Medical records prove what happened to the client's body. They are the objective evidence of harm.

⏱️ Establish Causation

Timeline from accident to treatment shows the connection between the crash and the injuries.

💰 Quantify Damages

Bills provide the economic foundation for the demand package. No bills = no damages number.

🛡️ Counter Defense Arguments

Complete records prevent "pre-existing condition" claims and gaps the defense can exploit.

The System Principle

🔧 The records process must work independently of any individual. If records retrieval depends on any person remembering to follow up, the system has failed. The 14-day follow-up rule and CasePeer tracking create the "franchise prototype" for records retrieval — any qualified team member should be able to maintain it.

What Happens Without Complete Records

⚠️ Missing records = reduced case value. Insurance adjusters use gaps in the medical record to argue injuries weren't serious, treatment wasn't necessary, or the client recovered sooner than claimed. Every missing provider is money left on the table.

Letter of Representation (LOR) — The Protection System

What an LOR Does

📄
The LOR formally protects the client from the moment we sign them. It notifies insurers that GLG represents the client and that all contact must go through us. Without it, adjusters contact clients directly within hours of an accident.

Legal Notice

Formally notifies the insurer that GLG represents the client.

Communication Control

All insurer contact must go through us after LOR receipt.

Client Protection

Prevents adjusters from getting recorded statements or pressuring quick settlements.

LOR Timeline & Process

1
Within 24–48 hours of signing the retainer
As soon as the client signs, the LOR must go out. Every day without an LOR allows the insurer to contact the client directly and potentially damage the case.
2
Send to ALL relevant insurers
At-fault driver's liability carrier, client's UM/UIM carrier, and MedPay carrier if applicable. Don't miss any — each can legally contact the client until they receive an LOR.
3
Use CasePeer LOR Templates
Template 01 (LOR At-Fault) — send to at-fault driver's liability carrier. Template 02 (LOR UM) — send to client's UM/UIM carrier. Access: Case → Documents → Templates.
4
Confirm receipt and log in CasePeer
Follow up within 5–7 days to confirm the insurer received and acknowledged the LOR. Document the confirmation date and contact person in CasePeer notes.

What Happens Without an LOR

🚨 Insurance adjusters will immediately contact unrepresented clients to get recorded statements — often within 24–48 hours of the accident. These statements are used to minimize claims later. Once a client gives a recorded statement without representation, it becomes much harder to achieve full case value.

Letter of Protection (LOP) — Treatment Access Strategy

What LOPs Enable

🏥 LOPs solve the client's real problem. Clients don't just want money — they want to get better and return to their normal life. A Letter of Protection makes treatment possible without upfront payment, which serves both the legal case and the client's actual needs.

Treatment Without Upfront Payment

Clients get care they can't afford to pay for immediately. The provider treats on credit, guaranteed by the settlement.

Case Development

More treatment = more documentation = higher case value. LOPs allow cases to develop fully.

Provider Relationships

LOPs help us build networks with treatment providers who regularly work with PI cases.

How LOPs Work

💰
The LOP creates a medical lien
When a provider treats under an LOP, they gain a legal right (lien) to be paid from settlement proceeds. This lien must be tracked and satisfied at disbursement.
📋
Both client and firm must sign
The LOP is a three-party agreement: client (receives treatment), provider (gives treatment on credit), law firm (guarantees payment from settlement). Use Template 12 in CasePeer.
🎯
Common LOP providers
Chiropractors, orthopedic surgeons, pain management doctors, imaging centers, physical therapy clinics. Emergency rooms typically don't work on LOPs.
💸
Negotiate liens at settlement
LOP providers often charge higher rates than insurance-negotiated rates. At settlement, we negotiate lien reductions to maximize the client's net recovery.

LOR vs. LOP — Don't Confuse Them

⚠️
LOR = Letter of Representation → sent to insurance companies
LOP = Letter of Protection → sent to medical providers
Different recipients, different purposes. Both are critical early steps in case management.

The Records Retrieval System

The Franchise Prototype Principle

🔧 The records process must work consistently for any staff member. Following this system ensures no provider gets missed, no follow-up gets forgotten, and every case develops the medical record it needs for maximum value — regardless of who handles the case.

Step-by-Step Records Request Process

1
Get signed HIPAA authorization (Template 44)
HIPAA compliance requires specific written authorization before providers can release records. Use Template 44 (Medical Authorization) from CasePeer. One authorization typically covers all providers, but some require separate forms.
2
Identify EVERY provider
Emergency room, urgent care, primary care, specialists, chiropractors, physical therapists, imaging centers, pharmacies. Always ask: "Who else did you see for this accident?" Don't assume you got them all in the first interview.
3
Request records AND bills together
Use Template 04 (Medical Records Request — Full) for complete records + bills, or Template 05 (Medical Bills Request) for billing only. Records document injuries; bills document economic damages. Both are required for the demand package.
4
Log and set 14-day follow-up in CasePeer
Document the request date in CasePeer and immediately set a 14-day follow-up task. For pre-litigation cases, every outstanding records request gets a follow-up every 14 days — no exceptions. This is the GLG standard.
5
Follow up every 14 days (Template 74)
Log every follow-up call with date, time, and contact person. Use Template 74 (Follow-Up Records Request) for written follow-ups. After three follow-ups with no response, escalate to the CM for more aggressive action.

The 14-Day Rule & Never Miss Twice

🚨 14-Day Rule: Every outstanding records request gets a follow-up every 14 days. No exceptions. Log every follow-up call in CasePeer with date, time, and contact person.
⚠️ Never Miss Twice: One missed follow-up is a mistake. Two in a row is a bad habit forming. Consistency prevents system breakdown and cases from stalling.

What to Include in Every Records Request

✅ Signed HIPAA authorization (Template 44)

✅ Client name, DOB, and SSN

✅ Accident date and date range of treatment

✅ Request for both records AND itemized billing

✅ GLG firm letterhead and contact information

✅ Instructions for delivery (mail, fax, secure email)

CasePeer Templates & The GLG Way

Why Templates Are Standards, Not Shortcuts

📋 Templates are how we systematize excellence. Every GLG letter should look professional, be legally compliant, and follow the same format. Templates eliminate variability and ensure consistency across all staff members — whether Harrell sends a letter or a new assistant does.

Core Templates for Records & LORs

#Template NameWhen to Use
01LOR At-FaultSend to at-fault insurance to open claim
02LOR UMSend to client's UM carrier to open UM claim
04Medical Records Request (Full)Request complete records + bills from provider
05Medical Bills RequestRequest bills only
12LOP (Letter of Protection)Guarantee payment to provider from settlement
44Medical Authorization (HIPAA)Client signs to authorize records release
74Follow-Up Records RequestFollow up on records not received

How to Use Templates in CasePeer

📂
Access templates
Go to case → Documents → Templates. Select the template by number or name. Templates are organized by function and numbered for easy reference.
✏️
Fill in variable fields
Client name, accident date, provider name, insurance information — complete all bracketed fields. Never send a template with blank fields or [PLACEHOLDER] text.
👁️
Review before sending
Double-check addresses, spelling, dates, and client details. A professional letter with a typo looks unprofessional. Take 30 seconds to review every template.
📤
Send and document in CasePeer
Print, mail, or email as appropriate. Upload a copy to CasePeer and log the sending date. Set follow-up tasks as needed.

Template Rules

⚠️ Don't modify templates without approval. If you think a template needs changes, bring it to the CM or Harrell. Templates are carefully designed for legal compliance and professional standards — ad hoc changes can create problems.
