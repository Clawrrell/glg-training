Section 3 · Hospital Lien Law · Up to 40 pts

Georgia Hospital Lien Statute

O.C.G.A. §§ 44-14-470 through 44-14-476 — five rules every LA must know cold.

⚖️ The Five Rules

#RuleStatute
1Authorized filers only: hospitals, nursing homes, physician practices, chiropractic practices, traumatic burn care centers. Private ambulance companies, urgent care (stand-alone), and other providers are NOT on the list.§ 44-14-470
215-day certified mail notice required before filing — regular mail does NOT satisfy this requirement.§ 44-14-471(a)(1)
3Filing deadlines: hospitals/nursing homes/burn care = 75 days from discharge. Physician/chiropractic practices = 90 days. This distinction is the most commonly confused rule in lien review.§ 44-14-471(a)(2)
4Non-compliance = automatically unenforceable. No court order needed. Any procedural defect voids the lien.§ 44-14-471(b)
52023 Rule: Health insurance must be billed first. Lien is only enforceable for amounts insurance did NOT cover. (2023 Ga. Laws 253)§ 44-14-471(c)
Additional rules: Attorney lien priority — § 44-14-470(b) establishes that the attorney's lien for fees has priority over hospital liens. Enforcement deadline — § 44-14-473(a): the hospital has 1 year from the date of settlement or judgment to enforce the lien, or it is waived.
75 vs. 90: Confusing these two deadlines is the most common lien review error. Hospitals/nursing homes/burn care = 75 days. Physician practices/chiropractors = 90 days. Memorize the distinction — one day past the deadline = lien void.

Section 4 · GSCCCA Verification · Up to 40 pts

Filed Lien vs. Billing Claim

Two completely different legal realities. One search tells you which one you have.

🔍 The Critical Distinction

Filed lien (on GSCCCA): Creates a legal obligation to hold funds. You cannot disburse without resolving it.

Billing claim (not filed): A debt the client owes — but NOT a legal hold on settlement proceeds. The client can disburse and pay the provider from their own funds.

📋 GSCCCA Search Process

  1. Go to www.gsccca.org → "Search Records" → "Lien Search"
  2. Select the county where the hospital/provider is located
  3. Search by client name: Last Name, First Name
  4. Look for entries categorized as "Hospital Lien" or "Medical Lien"
  5. Note: filing date, provider name, and lien amount
  6. Log one row per provider in CasePeer: Provider | Filed Lien? | Amount | GSCCCA Reference

⚖️ Decision Flowchart

IF filed lien found: Hold lien amount + 10–15% cushion in trust; begin negotiation; do NOT disburse the held amount.

IF no filed lien: Have client sign Self-Pay Addendum acknowledging they are responsible for the unfiled balance → disburse.

Partial Disbursement Rule: Hold ONLY the filed lien amounts. Disburse everything else immediately. No client should wait months with zero funds while liens are negotiated.

Aspirion and Synergi: These collection companies frequently send letters claiming to represent hospitals and asserting payment rights. A collection letter from Aspirion or Synergi is NOT the same as a filed lien. Always verify on GSCCCA first — never hold or pay based solely on a collection letter.

Section 5 · Lien Negotiation · Up to 40 pts

The Seven-Stage Lien Negotiation Framework

Every dollar reduced from a lien goes to the client. No lien gets paid at face value without going through the framework.

⚖️ GLG's Seven-Stage Framework

StageAction
1 — PrepKnow total settlement, all liens, fees/costs, and made-whole threshold before making first contact
2 — ContactInitial contact within 48 hours of settlement; confirm lien amount and billing contact
3 — Anchor HighRequest maximum reduction first — 1/3 is the floor, not the ceiling
4 — Pro-RataPresent documented math: Available funds ÷ Total bills = Pro-Rata percentage; apply to each provider
5 — Made WholeIf liens + fees exceed recovery, document and assert Made Whole (for eligible lienholders)
6 — Loss Aversion"40 cents guaranteed today vs. zero and years of litigation" — loss aversion framing
7 — EscalateIf lienholder refuses reasonable reduction, escalate to Pre-Lit Head Attorney immediately

📐 Pro-Rata Calculation

Formula: Available Funds ÷ Total Billed = Pro-Rata %

Available Funds = Settlement − Attorney Fees − Case Costs

Apply the percentage to each provider's specific lien/bill amount.

When to just pay: Liens under $500 may not justify the time cost of negotiation — confirm with supervising attorney.

Written confirmation required: Before ANY disbursement to a provider, you must have written confirmation that the agreed-upon amount is accepted as payment in full. Verbal agreements are not binding. Written confirmation = the only protection.
Common Fund Doctrine: Every lienholder owes their pro-rata share of attorney fees for creating the fund from which they will be paid. This applies even to ERISA plans (where Made Whole may be unavailable).

Section 6 · Government & Health Insurance Liens · Up to 40 pts

Medicare, ERISA & Health Insurance Subrogation

Three categories. The category determines the doctrine.

📋 The Three-Category Framework

CategoryTypeRule
1 — MUST ADDRESSMedicare, Medicaid, ERISA self-funded plansResolve before any disbursement. No exceptions. Federal law. Disbursing without resolving Medicare = personal attorney liability.
2 — MADE WHOLEFully insured non-ERISA health plansGeorgia law applies. Duncan v. Integon, 267 Ga. 646 (1997). Send Template 26 (Made Whole Letter) by certified mail. 30-day wait. No response = claim effectively waived.
3 — DOCUMENTFully insured plans where client IS made wholeIf recovery fully compensates — document the Made Whole analysis and proceed.

🏥 Medicare — Category 1

  • Contact: BCRC (Benefits Coordination & Recovery Center) — 1-855-798-2627
  • Request the conditional payment letter early — before demand goes out if possible
  • Use the MSPRP (Medicare Secondary Payer Recovery Portal) for disputes
  • Made Whole Doctrine does NOT apply to Medicare — it is federal law
  • GSCCCA is irrelevant for Medicare — this is not a state hospital lien

🏛 ERISA Self-Funded Plans — Category 1 (with Common Fund)

ERISA preempts Georgia's Made Whole Doctrine (29 U.S.C. § 1132). Template 26 does NOT apply. But the Common Fund Doctrine survives in federal court: Lien Amount ÷ Total Recovery × Attorney Fees = Plan's share of fees owed. Typical result: ~33% automatic reduction.

📝 How to Identify ERISA vs. Fully Insured

Call the number on the back of the insurance card and ask: "Is this plan self-funded by the employer, or is it fully insured through [carrier name]?"

Document: rep's name, date, and answer in CasePeer. The insurance card does NOT reliably state plan type.

Partial Disbursement with 15% cushion: Hold estimated lien + 15% cushion. Disburse everything else. Formula: Estimated Lien × 1.15 = Trust Hold. Client receives: Settlement − Fees − Costs − Trust Hold.
NEVER disburse without resolving Medicare. The attorney faces federal personal liability for improper disbursement. This is the highest-stakes lien type in PI practice.
